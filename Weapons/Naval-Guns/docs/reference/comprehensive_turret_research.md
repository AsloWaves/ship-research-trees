# Comprehensive Historical Naval Gun Turret Research
## Complete Turret Database Reference (1890s - 1950s)

This document catalogs all historical naval gun turrets from the Big 4 nations (USA, Britain, Germany, Japan) organized chronologically and by ship type.

## PRE-DREADNOUGHT ERA (1890s-1906)

### Britain - Royal Navy

**Majestic-class Battleship Turrets (1895)**
- **Designation**: BL 12-inch Mk VIII BII/BIII mountings
- **Guns per turret**: 2
- **Caliber**: 12-inch (305mm)
- **Elevation**: -5° to +13.5°
- **Turrets**: 2 (one forward, one aft)
- **Ships**: Majestic, Caesar, Illustrious, Prince George, Victorious, Jupiter, Hannibal, Mars, Magnificent
- **Notes**: First fully-enclosed rotating turrets in Royal Navy. BII mountings required centerline loading, BIII had all-around loading

- **Notes**: Built in Britain (Kongo) or under British license. Later modernized to +33° elevation

**Fuso-class Battleship Turrets (1915)**

- **Turret weight**: 615 long tons
- **Turrets**: 6 (2-1-1-2 arrangement)
- **Ships**: Fuso, Yamashiro
- **Notes**: Six twin turrets requiring longer ship. Amidships turrets had restricted arcs

## INTERWAR PERIOD (1918-1939)

### Britain - Royal Navy

**Nelson-class Battleship Turrets (1927)**
- **Designation**: BL 16-inch Mk I turrets
- **Guns per turret**: 3
- **Caliber**: 16-inch (406mm)
- **Elevation**: Unknown (estimated +40°)
- **Turrets**: 3 (all forward: A and B superfiring, X on forecastle)
- **Turret armor**: Face 16in, sides 11in, roof 7.25in, rear 9in, barbettes 15in
- **Ships**: Nelson, Rodney
- **Notes**: Unique all-forward arrangement. X turret could not fire directly forward or aft. Demonstrated 33 consecutive rounds in 1929

**County-class Heavy Cruiser Turrets (1928)**
- **Designation**: BL 8-inch Mk VIII twin turrets
- **Guns per turret**: 2
- **Caliber**: 8-inch (203mm) L/50
- **Elevation**: 0° to +70°

- **Turret armor**: 1-inch (25mm) splinter plating
- **Ships**: Kent, Berwick, Cornwall, Cumberland, Suffolk, Norfolk, Dorsetshire, London, Devonshire, Shropshire, Sussex
- **Notes**: Designed for AA capability but lacked proper fire control. Needlessly complicated design
