## üéØ Classification Tier Structure

### **Tier 1: Basic Classification** (Foundation Specializations)
**Unlock Requirement**: Crew card reaches **Level 25**
**Unlock Cost**: **75,000‚Ç° per classification**
**Design Rationale**:
- Unlocks during T2-T3 ship ownership (early-mid progression)
- Cost equivalent to ~2-3 successful T3 missions
- Commitment point where neutral crew becomes specialized
- Accessible but meaningful investment for new players

**Available Classifications**:
- Gunner
- AA Specialist
- Torpedoman
- Engineer
- Damage Control Specialist
- Helmsman
- Command Officer

---

### **Tier 2: Advanced Specialization** (Mid-Game Mastery)
**Unlock Requirement**: Crew card reaches **Level 80**
**Unlock Cost**: **500,000‚Ç° per specialization**
**Design Rationale**:
- Unlocks during T5-T6 ship ownership (competitive tier)
- Cost equivalent to ~1 successful T6 mission
- Represents serious commitment to specialization path
- Level 80 = ~150-200 hours of active gameplay with that crew

**Prerequisite**: Must have Tier 1 Basic Classification unlocked first

---

### **Tier 3: Elite Specialization** (Endgame Excellence)
**Unlock Requirement**: Crew card reaches **Level 150**
**Unlock Cost**: **2,500,000‚Ç° per elite specialization**
**Design Rationale**:
- Unlocks during T8-T9 ship ownership (elite tier)
- Cost equivalent to ~2-3 T8 missions or 1 T9 mission
- Represents peak mastery of specialization
- Level 150 = ~400+ hours with that crew card
- High permadeath risk zones make this extremely valuable

**Prerequisite**: Must have Tier 2 Advanced Specialization unlocked first

## üí° Design Balance Points

### **Economic Pressure**
- Elite crew cost = 1-3 missions at appropriate tier
- Creates meaningful choice: upgrade ship vs. crew vs. modules
- Loss of elite crew = significant setback but not game-ending
- Encourages diverse progression (multiple crew cards, multiple specializations)

### **Time Investment**
- 400-500 hours per elite crew = serious commitment
- Matches ship progression timelines (T1-T10 = 200-400 hours)
- Players reach endgame with 1-2 elite crew naturally
- Further crew specializations = dedicated long-term goals

### **Permadeath Integration**
- T6 permadeath = 15% risk, elite crew still viable
- T8 permadeath = 30% risk, elite crew high stakes
- T10 permadeath = 100% risk, elite crew extremely risky
- Creates natural risk/reward: use elite crew for advantage vs. preserve them

### **Strategic Depth**
- 7 basic classifications √ó 3 advanced per class √ó 3 elite per advanced = 63 total specializations
- Each ship requires 5-15 crew positions (depending on tier)
- Impossible to master everything = specialization encouraged
- Player identity formed through crew composition choices

- **Replacement** (dead crew card): Full unlock cost + 400-500 hours lost
