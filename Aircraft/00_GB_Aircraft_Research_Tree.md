# Royal Navy Fleet Air Arm Aircraft Research Tree

## Overview
Complete research tree for British Royal Navy carrier aircraft from 1915 to present day. Covers the evolution from WWI seaplanes through biplanes, WWII monoplanes, early jets, and modern VSTOL/stealth fighters.

## Technology Progression

### Propulsion Evolution
1. **Rotary Engines (1915-1925):** Le Rhône, Clerget, Bentley BR series
2. **In-Line Piston (1920-1950):** Napier Lion, Rolls-Royce Kestrel/Merlin
3. **Radial Piston (1930-1950):** Bristol Pegasus, Armstrong Siddeley
4. **Turbojets (1947-1960):** Goblin, Nene, Avon
5. **Turbofans (1962-Present):** Spey, Pegasus (VSTOL), F135

### Armament Evolution
1. **WWI (1915-1920):** Single .303 Lewis/Vickers MG, small bombs
2. **Interwar (1920-1938):** 2x .303 MGs, 2000 lb torpedo capability
3. **WWII (1939-1945):** 4-8x .303 MGs or 20mm cannon, rockets, guided weapons
4. **Early Jets (1947-1960):** 20mm/30mm cannon, early AAMs (Firestreak)
5. **Modern (1962-Present):** Precision guided munitions, BVRAAMs, stealth weapons carriage

### Carrier Operations Evolution
1. **WWI:** Platforms on battleships, seaplane carriers, first deck landings
2. **Interwar:** Purpose-built carriers, arresting hooks standard
3. **WWII:** Catapults introduced, larger air groups
4. **Cold War:** Angled decks, steam catapults, mirror landing systems
5. **Modern:** VSTOL operations, ski-jumps, no catapults needed (Sea Harrier, F-35B)

## Notable Achievements

### WWI Firsts
- **First torpedo sinking from aircraft:** Short 184 (1915)
- **First landing on moving ship:** Sopwith Pup on HMS Furious (Aug 2, 1917)
- **First purpose-designed carrier aircraft:** Fairey Campania (1917)

### WWII Highlights
- **Taranto Raid (Nov 1940):** Swordfish crippled Italian fleet
- **Sinking of Bismarck (May 1941):** Swordfish torpedo hit jammed rudder
- **Tirpitz Attack (Apr 1944):** Barracuda dive bombing (Operation Tungsten)

### Jet Era Milestones
- **First carrier jet landing:** Sea Vampire, HMS Ocean (Dec 3, 1945)
- **First operational jet fighter:** Supermarine Attacker (1951)
- **Suez Crisis (1956):** Sea Hawk ground attack operations

### Modern Operations
- **Falklands War (1982):** Sea Harrier - 87% kill rate (21 of 24), zero air-to-air losses
- **F-35B IOC:** December 2018, reformed 617 Squadron "The Dambusters"
- **Queen Elizabeth Operations:** First F-35B deployment HMS Queen Elizabeth (2021)
