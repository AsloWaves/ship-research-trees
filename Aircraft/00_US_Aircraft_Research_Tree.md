# US Navy Carrier Aircraft Research Tree

## Overview
Complete research tree for United States Navy carrier aircraft from 1911 to present day. Covers the evolution from pioneer flying boats through WWI scouts, WWII legends, Cold War jets, to modern fifth-generation stealth fighters.

**Total Aircraft:** 35 aircraft spanning 108 years (1911-2019)

## Fire Control Generations

### Generation 0: Manual (1911-1935)
- Manual sights (iron/ring)
- No electrical systems
- Mechanical interrupter gear (WWI)
- **Aircraft:** 14 aircraft (40%)

### Generation 1: Basic Electrical (1936-1949)
- Electrical gun synchronization
- Reflector gun sights
- Radio communication
- Radar (late variants)
- **Aircraft:** 10 aircraft (29%)

### Generation 2: Analog Fire Control (1956-1974)
- Gyroscopic gun sights
- Analog fire control computers
- Radar ranging and lock
- Semi-active radar missiles
- **Aircraft:** 7 aircraft (20%)

### Generation 3: Digital Systems (1983-Present)
- Digital fire control computers
- AESA radars (F-35C)
- Databus integration (MIL-STD-1553, 1760)
- Precision weapon delivery
- Sensor fusion (F-35C)
- **Aircraft:** 4 aircraft (11%)

## Technology Progression

### Propulsion Evolution
1. **Rotary/Inline Piston (1911-1920):** Curtiss OX-5, Liberty L-12
2. **Radial Piston (1920-1949):** Pratt & Whitney R-1340, R-2800
3. **Turbojets (1949-1970):** J48, J57, J79
4. **Turbofans (1970-Present):** TF30, F404, F414, F135

### Armament Evolution
1. **WWI (1911-1920):** None or 1x .30 cal MG, small bombs
2. **Interwar (1927-1936):** 2x .30/.50 cal, bombs, torpedoes
3. **WWII (1940-1949):** 4-8x .50 cal, 20mm cannon, torpedoes, rockets, bombs
4. **Cold War (1956-1974):** 20mm cannon, Sidewinder, Sparrow, bombs, Bullpup
5. **Modern (1983-Present):** 20mm M61, AMRAAM, Sidewinder, PGMs, JDAMs, HARMs

### Carrier Operations Evolution
1. **Pioneer (1911-1922):** Launched by catapult from battleships, cranes recover
2. **Interwar (1922-1941):** USS Langley first carrier, arresting wires
3. **WWII (1941-1945):** Fast carriers, mass production, island superstructure
4. **Cold War (1945-1991):** Angled decks, steam catapults, mirror landing, nuclear carriers
5. **Modern (1991-Present):** Electromagnetic catapults (EMALS), advanced arresting, F-35C stealth

## Notable Achievements

### Pioneer Firsts
- **First Navy aircraft:** A-1 Triad (1911)
- **First transatlantic flight:** NC-4 (May 1919)
- **First carrier takeoff:** VE-7S from USS Langley (Oct 17, 1922)

### WWII Highlights
- **Battle of Midway (June 1942):** SBD Dauntless sank 4 Japanese carriers
- **Hellcat dominance:** 5,223 kills, 19:1 kill ratio, 75% of Navy air-to-air kills
- **Corsair legend:** 2,140 kills, 11:1 ratio, "Whistling Death"
- **Avenger versatility:** Torpedoes, bombs, rockets - George HW Bush flew TBM

### Cold War Milestones
- **Korea:** F9F Panther first Navy jet in combat
- **Vietnam:** F-4 Phantom II workhorse, A-6 all-weather attacks
- **Mach 2:** F-4 Phantom (Mach 2.23), F-14 Tomcat (Mach 2.34)
- **Phoenix missile:** F-14 only aircraft to fire AIM-54, 100+ mile range

### Modern Operations
- **Gulf War (1991):** F/A-18 Hornet, A-6 Intruder precision strikes
- **Libya (2011):** EA-18G Growler combat debut
- **F-35C IOC:** February 2019 (VFA-147 "Argonauts")
- **Current:** Super Hornet backbone, F-35C integration ongoing
