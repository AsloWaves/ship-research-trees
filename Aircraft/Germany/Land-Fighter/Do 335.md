## History

The Dornier Do 335 Pfeil was one of the fastest piston-engine fighters ever built and featured one of aviation's most unusual configurations: a centerline thrust arrangement with engines in both the nose and tail driving separate propellers in a push-pull configuration. This unique layout eliminated the asymmetric thrust problems of twin-engine aircraft while providing exceptional speed and performance.

Claude Dornier had experimented with push-pull configurations since the 1930s, but the Do 335 represented the first successful operational application. The centerline thrust concept allowed the aircraft to combine the power of two engines with the aerodynamic efficiency of a single-engine design. The result was extraordinary: the Do 335 was faster than the Fw 190D and nearly as fast as the Me 262 jet, while having far better range and endurance.

However, the Do 335 arrived too late to impact the war. The first prototype flew in October 1943, but development problems, Allied bombing of factories, and shifting priorities delayed production. Only about 37 aircraft were completed before war's end, and very few saw combat. Those that did demonstrated impressive performance, but there were too few to matter. The innovative design influenced post-war aircraft development, and the centerline thrust concept was validated, though it remained rare due to complexity. Test pilots praised the Do 335's speed, handling, and firepower, noting it could have been a formidable opponent had it entered service earlier in significant numbers.

## Notable Features

- Unique centerline push-pull engine configuration
- One of the fastest piston-engine aircraft ever built
- Cruciform tail with rear propeller mounted in fuselage
- Ejection seat (rare for German aircraft)
- Rear propeller had explosive-bolt separation for emergency bailout
- Tricycle landing gear
- Exceptional speed combined with heavy armament
- Could engage and disengage from combat at will due to speed
- Tandem cockpit in two-seat trainer version (Do 335A-12)
