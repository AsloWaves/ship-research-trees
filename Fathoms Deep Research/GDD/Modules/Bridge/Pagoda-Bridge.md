## Description

Distinctive multi-tiered superstructure unique to Japanese capital ships, resembling a Buddhist pagoda. Features multiple fire control platforms, rangefinder positions, and command stations stacked vertically. Provided excellent optical fire control but created top-weight and blast interference problems.

## Historical Notes

The pagoda mast evolved through the 1920s-30s as the IJN added more fire control equipment to existing ships. Fuso-class battleships had the most extreme pagodas, reaching over 40 meters in height. While providing excellent optical fire control, the pagoda created problems: top-heaviness required adding torpedo bulges for stability, main gun blast damaged the lower platforms, and the structure became a distinctive recognition feature. By the late war, surviving ships had their pagodas simplified and radar added.
