## Description

Revolutionary streamlined conning tower for the Type XXI "Elektroboot" - the world's first true submarine designed to operate primarily underwater. Dramatically reduced profile, integrated snorkel, advanced hydraulics, and radar systems. Too late to impact WWII, but influenced all post-war submarine design.

## Historical Notes

The Type XXI Elektroboot represents one of history's great "what-ifs." Had it been deployed in 1943 instead of 1945, the Battle of the Atlantic's outcome might have been very different. The Type XXI's ability to outrun escorts underwater, attack without surfacing, and operate indefinitely with its snorkel made Allied ASW tactics obsolete. But Germany's collapse came before the new boats could be properly deployed. The ultimate irony: while Type XXI had virtually no impact on WWII, it won the post-war battle - every submarine since has followed its revolutionary design. The Elektroboot lost WWII but won the future.
