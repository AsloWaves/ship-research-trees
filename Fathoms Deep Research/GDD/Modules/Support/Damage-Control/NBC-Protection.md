## Description

Comprehensive Nuclear, Biological, and Chemical warfare protection system providing collective protection for crew. Features include filtered air ventilation, citadel overpressure, contamination detection, decontamination showers, and sealed compartment systems. Enables ship operations in NBC-contaminated environments where unprotected ships cannot function.

## Effect on Gameplay

Provides immunity to NBC weapons that would disable unprotected ships. Crew remains combat-effective in contaminated environments. Ship can transit through radioactive fallout, chemical weapon clouds, or biological agent areas. Essential for operations in NBC threat environments or after nuclear weapon use.

## System Components

### Collective Protection Citadel
- **Sealed compartments**: Gas-tight critical spaces
- **Overpressure system**: Prevents contamination entry
- **Filtered air supply**: HEPA and chemical filters
- **Independent ventilation**: Isolated from external air
- **Combat spaces**: Bridge, CIC, weapons control protected
- **Living spaces**: Crew accommodation included
- **Medical facilities**: Treatment under protection

### Detection Systems
- **Radiation detectors**: Gamma, neutron monitoring
- **Chemical sensors**: Nerve and blister agent detection
- **Biological sensors**: Aerosol particle analysis
- **Continuous monitoring**: Real-time threat assessment
- **Alarm systems**: Automatic NBC alerts
- **Contamination mapping**: Track contaminated areas

### Ventilation System
- **HEPA filters**: 99.97% particulate removal
- **Chemical filters**: Activated carbon beds
- **Pre-filters**: Extended filter life
- **Overpressure fans**: Maintain positive pressure
- **Automatic valves**: Seal on contamination detection
- **Backup systems**: Redundant fans and filters

### Decontamination Systems
- **Wash-down stations**: External hull decontamination
- **Personnel showers**: Crew decontamination
- **Equipment wash**: Decontaminate tools/weapons
- **Drain collection**: Contain contaminated water
- **Hot spots treatment**: Concentrate on high-contamination areas

## Threat Protection

### Nuclear Weapons

#### Initial Effects
- **Flash/Heat**: Crew protected inside ship
- **Blast**: Ship structure provides protection
- **EMP**: Hardened electronics needed (separate system)
- **Prompt radiation**: Ship structure attenuates

#### Fallout
- **Particulate radiation**: Filters remove completely
- **External contamination**: Wash-down systems clean
- **Duration**: Ship can operate indefinitely in fallout
- **Personnel**: No exposure inside citadel

### Chemical Weapons

#### Nerve Agents (VX, Sarin, Tabun)
- **Filtration**: 100% removal by chemical filters
- **Detection**: Instant alarm on trace amounts
- **Protection**: Complete inside citadel
- **Decontamination**: Wash-down neutralizes

#### Blister Agents (Mustard, Lewisite)
- **Filtration**: Complete protection
- **External contamination**: Wash-down required
- **Persistence**: Agent remains on surfaces
- **Protection**: Crew safe inside, cautious outside

#### Blood Agents (Cyanide)
- **Filtration**: Activated carbon removes
- **Detection**: Chemical sensors alert
- **Lethality**: Extremely dangerous without protection
- **Protection**: Citadel overpressure prevents entry

### Biological Weapons

#### Bacterial Agents (Anthrax, Plague)
- **Filtration**: HEPA removes 99.97% of particles
- **Detection**: Biological aerosol sensors
- **Medical**: Treatment possible if exposure occurs
- **Decontamination**: Thorough disinfection required

#### Viral Agents (Smallpox, etc.)
- **Filtration**: HEPA effective
- **Transmission**: Person-to-person concerns
- **Medical**: Isolation protocols
- **Protection**: Prevents aerosol infection

#### Toxins (Ricin, Botulinum)
- **Filtration**: Removed by filters
- **Potency**: Extremely lethal
- **Detection**: Difficult to identify quickly
- **Protection**: Citadel prevents exposure

## Historical Notes

NBC protection emerged during the Cold War as nuclear weapons proliferated and chemical/biological threats materialized. Early systems were basic - ventilation shutdown and crew in protective gear. The USN developed citadel overpressure concepts in the 1950s, recognizing crew protection was essential for post-nuclear operations.

The Falklands War emphasized basics - British ships sealed ventilation during fires to prevent smoke intake, demonstrating NBC-like procedures. Modern ships like the DDG-51 have sophisticated NBC protection enabling operations after nuclear exchange.

Cold War exercises tested NBC procedures. Ships sealed up, operated under citadel protection, and practiced decontamination. The procedures proved complex and demanding - maintaining seal integrity while fighting the ship requires discipline and training.

The 1991 Gulf War saw NBC protection tested. Iraqi chemical weapons were a threat, and all Coalition ships maintained NBC readiness. The threat proved unrealized, but preparedness was essential. Later operations saw renewed emphasis as regional powers developed chemical weapons.

Modern systems integrate NBC protection with general damage control. The same overpressure system that protects against nerve gas also helps with shipboard fires. Decontamination systems double as firefighting equipment. The result is comprehensive survivability systems addressing multiple threats.
