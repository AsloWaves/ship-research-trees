## Description
Elevated observation platform mounted high on the mainmast or foremast. The increased height extends the visual horizon, allowing earlier detection of distant vessels. Historically the primary lookout position on sailing vessels, it remained valuable on steam warships despite the exposure and discomfort for the crew.

## Historical Notes
The crow's nest dates to the age of sail, when lookouts climbed to platforms high on the masts. Even on steel battleships, elevated lookouts provided crucial early warning. HMS Hood's lookouts spotted Bismarck from the foretop. However, the exposed position was dangerous - spray, wind, and cold made it a miserable posting. Japanese lookouts in elevated positions were legendary for their keen eyesight and endurance.
