## Description
Heavy naval searchlight with 60-inch (152cm) diameter mirror, typically mounted on capital ships and large cruisers. Projects an extremely powerful beam capable of illuminating targets at considerable distance. The largest practical searchlight size for naval use, providing maximum night-fighting capability before radar.

## Historical Notes
The 60-inch searchlight represented the peak of naval illumination technology. British battleships and cruisers mounted these massive lights for night actions. HMS Hood carried multiple 60-inch searchlights. The Imperial Japanese Navy also invested heavily in large searchlights for their night-fighting doctrine. However, combat experience quickly showed the deadly risk - activating searchlights made ships easy targets. At the Battle of Cape Matapan (1941), Italian cruisers illuminated by British searchlights were devastated by point-blank fire. By mid-WWII, radar made searchlights obsolete for surface combat, though they remained useful for signaling and anti-aircraft illumination.
