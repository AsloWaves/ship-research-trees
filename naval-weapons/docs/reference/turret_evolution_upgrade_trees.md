### **Upgrade Tree Structure**

1. **Linear Progressions**: Most turrets follow a clear Mark I â†’ Mark II â†’ Mark III path
2. **Branching Options**: Some ships can choose between twin/triple configurations
3. **Modernization Events**: Trigger major upgrades at historical dates (1920s, 1930s, 1940s)

### **Key Game Mechanics**

**Tonnage System**:
- Each ship has maximum turret weight capacity
- Heavier turrets may require structural upgrades (tonnage penalty)
- Triple turrets are more weight-efficient than twin (better tons-per-gun ratio)

**Upgrade Requirements**:
- Tech level requirements (year unlocks)
- Resource costs scale with tier
- Higher tiers require rare materials or long refit times

**Performance Scaling**:
- Elevation affects maximum range
- Rate of fire affects DPS
- Fire control affects accuracy
- Armor penetration scales with shell weight and velocity

**Historical Accuracy**:
- Ships can only mount turrets they historically could use (or similar weight/size)
- Modernization paths follow historical precedent
- Concept turrets (Montana, H-class, A-150) are rare "legendary" end-game options
