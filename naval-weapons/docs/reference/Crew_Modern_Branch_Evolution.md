# CREW CLASSIFICATION MODERN BRANCH EVOLUTION
## WOS2.3 Naval MMO - WWII to Modern Era Specializations

### EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Purpose**: Extend the crew classification system from WWII era (1940s) into Cold War (1950s-1980s) and Modern era (1990s-2020s) to support late-game content with guided missiles, nuclear power, AEGIS systems, and advanced electronics.

**Design Philosophy**: Natural evolution from existing WWII classifications with technology prerequisites, maintaining Navy Field-style progression while adding depth for endgame players.

**Key Concepts**:
- **Branching Points**: Levels 150, 200, 240 unlock modern specializations
- **Technology Prerequisites**: Ship must have appropriate systems (missiles, nuclear reactor, AEGIS, etc.)
- **Dual Relevance**: Some WWII roles remain viable, others obsolete, some transform
- **New Divisions**: Missile Systems, Electronic Warfare, Computer Operations

---

## 1. BRANCHING SYSTEM OVERVIEW

### Era Definitions

**WWII Era** (1940s)
- Gun-based warfare
- Manual fire control
- Conventional propulsion
- Analog systems
- Large crews (1,500-2,500 on battleships)

**Cold War Era** (1950s-1980s)
- Guided missiles introduced
- Nuclear propulsion
- Early radar/sonar automation
- Transition to electronics
- Medium crews (300-500 on cruisers)

**Modern Era** (1990s-2020s)
- AEGIS combat systems
- Vertical launch systems (VLS)
- Phased array radar
- Network-centric warfare
- Small crews (200-300 on destroyers)

### Branching Mechanics

**Level 150 Branch** - Cold War Transition
- Requires: Chief-level WWII classification (Level 150+)
- Technology: Ship must have Cold War systems (guided missiles, early electronics)
- New Classifications: 18 Cold War specializations
- Progression: Level 150 → 180 → 210 (3 ranks)

**Level 200 Branch** - Modern Systems
- Requires: Veteran Cold War classification (Level 180+) OR Elite WWII Chief (Level 200+)
- Technology: Ship must have modern systems (AEGIS, VLS, advanced radar)
- New Classifications: 22 Modern specializations
- Progression: Level 200 → 230 → 260 (3 ranks)

**Level 240 Branch** - Ultimate Specialization
- Requires: Expert Modern classification (Level 230+)
- Technology: Ship must have cutting-edge systems (BMD, integrated networks)
- New Classifications: 8 Elite Modern specializations
- Progression: Level 240 → 270 → 300 (3 ranks - maximum)

## 2. TECHNOLOGY PREREQUISITES

### Ship Technology Tiers

**WWII Technology** (No special requirements)
- Gun turrets (3"-20" caliber)
- Torpedo tubes
- Manual fire control
- Conventional steam/diesel propulsion
- Basic radar (surface search, fire control)

**Cold War Technology** (Unlocks Level 150 branches)
- **Missile Systems**: SAMs (Terrier, Tartar), SSMs (Harpoon)
- **Nuclear Propulsion**: Reactors (submarine or surface)
- **Advanced Radar**: 3D air search, early automation
- **Improved Sonar**: Hull-mounted active/passive, early towed arrays
- **Gas Turbine Propulsion**: LM2500 turbines
- **Helicopter Facilities**: Flight deck, hangar

**Modern Technology** (Unlocks Level 200 branches)
- **AEGIS Combat System**: SPY-1 phased array radar, integrated fire control
- **VLS (Vertical Launch System)**: Mk 41/Mk 57 cells for multiple missile types
- **Advanced Sonar**: AN/SQS-53C, towed arrays (TB-23/29)
- **Digital Combat Systems**: Automated CIC, data links (Link 16)
- **Advanced Helicopters**: SH-60 Seahawk ASW/ASUW
- **Electronic Warfare**: AN/SLQ-32 ECM suite

**Cutting-Edge Technology** (Unlocks Level 240 branches)
- **Ballistic Missile Defense**: SM-3, BMD-capable AEGIS
- **Network-Centric Warfare**: CEC (Cooperative Engagement Capability)
- **Advanced VLS**: Multi-mission capability (AAW, ASW, Strike, BMD)
- **Integrated Power Systems**: Electric propulsion, directed energy weapons
- **Stealth Technology**: Reduced radar cross-section hulls
- **Unmanned Systems**: USVs, UUVs integration

## 5. OBSOLETE CLASSIFICATIONS

**Roles that become obsolete in modern era** (no modern branch available):

2. **Hedgehog Operator** - Completely replaced by ASROC/VLA

## 7. TECHNOLOGY-GATED PROGRESSION

### Ship Technology Requirements Summary

**To unlock Level 150 branches**, ship must have ONE of:
- Guided missiles (SAM or SSM)
- Nuclear propulsion
- Gas turbine engines
- ASW helicopters
- Advanced radar/sonar systems

**To unlock Level 200 branches**, ship must have ONE of:
- AEGIS combat system
- Mk 41 VLS (48+ cells)
- Advanced sonar (SQS-53C or better)
- Integrated power systems
- Network data links (Link 16)

**To unlock Level 240 branches**, ship must have ONE of:
- BMD-capable AEGIS
- Cooperative Engagement Capability (CEC)
- Multi-mission VLS (96+ cells)
- Integrated unmanned systems
- Directed energy weapons

### Crew Investment Comparison

- Ships: WWII vessels only
- Performance: 100% on WWII ships, 40-60% on modern ships (wrong specialty)

**Full Modern Crew** (Level 1→260):

- Ships: Cold War + Modern vessels
- Performance: 100% on modern ships, 80% on WWII ships (overqualified)

**Ultimate Crew** (Level 1→300):
- Time: 740 hours
- Cost: 30-50M credits
- Ships: Cutting-edge vessels only
- Performance: 130% on ultimate ships (synergy bonuses)

### Retraining vs Branching Decision

**Scenario**: Player has Level 190 Chief AA Gunner, wants to operate AEGIS cruiser

**Option 1: Branch existing crew**
- Start at Level 150, branch to SAM Operator
- Continue 150→260 (110 levels, ~220 hours, 11M credits)
- Retains WWII capability
- **Total Investment**: Level 1→260

**Option 2: Train new crew from scratch**
- Recruit new Neutral Sailor
- Train directly for modern role (no WWII branch)
- 1→260 but faster (no "wasted" WWII levels)
- **Total Investment**: ~500 hours, 18M credits
- **Trade-off**: No WWII capability

**Recommended**: Branch existing elite crew for versatility, train new crew for specialized modern-only roles
