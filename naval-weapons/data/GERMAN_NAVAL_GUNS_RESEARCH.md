### 2. 38cm/52 (15") SK C/34 - BISMARCK, TIRPITZ
**Gun ID**: 701

- **Length**: /52 (51.66 calibers German method, 48.43 bore length)
- **Year**: 1940
- **Weight**: 111.7 tons (110 long tons)
- **Muzzle Velocity**: 820 m/s (2,690 fps) with APC shell
- **Projectile Weight**: 800 kg (1,764 lbs) AP, 495 kg (1,091 lbs) HE
- **Rate of Fire**: 3 rpm per gun (verified in combat)
- **Barrel Life**: 250-300 EFC

- **Rifling**: 90 grooves, increasing twist
- **Ships**: Bismarck, Tirpitz (4 twin turrets each = 8 guns per ship)
- **Turret Weight**: 1,048-1,056 tonnes per turret
- **Turret Traverse**: 5.4°/sec, Elevation: 6°/sec
- **Elevation Range**: -5.5° to +30°
- **Max Range**: 36,520 m (39,930 yds) @ 30°
- **Notable**: Bismarck made multiple hits on HMS Hood at 22,000m. Tirpitz never fired main guns in anger.
- **Source**: NavWeaps WNGER_15-52_skc34

## LIGHT CRUISER / SECONDARY ARMAMENT

### 9. 15cm/55 (5.9") SK C/28 - MOST COMMON GERMAN CRUISER GUN
**Gun ID**: 708
- **Designation**: SK C/28
- **Caliber**: 15cm (5.9") - Actual: 149mm (5.87")
- **Length**: /55 (55 calibers) - Actually 57.84 calibers
- **Year**: 1933
- **Weight**: 8.585 tons (8.45 long tons)
- **Muzzle Velocity**: 960 m/s (3,150 fps) with L/4.7 shell, 875 m/s with L/4.4 HE
- **Projectile Weight**: 45.3 kg (100 lbs) L/4.7 AP, 45.5 kg HE
- **Rate of Fire**: 6-8 rpm
- **Barrel Life**: 1,100 rounds (800 EFC with L/4.7 shell)
- **Chamber Volume**: 13.2 liters
- **Rifling**: 44 grooves, uniform RH 1/30
- **Cartridge Case**: 9.15 kg (20 lbs) brass with 13.7 kg (30 lbs) propellant
- **Ships**:
  - **Light Cruisers**: Königsberg, Karlsruhe, Köln, Leipzig, Nürnberg (3 triple turrets = 9 guns)
  - **Secondary**: Bismarck, Tirpitz (6 twin turrets = 12 guns), Scharnhorst, Gneisenau (4 twin + 2 single = 10-12 guns)
  - **Pocket Battleships**: Deutschland-class (8 single mounts)
  - **Aircraft Carriers**: Graf Zeppelin-class (6 twin turrets = 12 guns planned)
- **Turret Weight**: 39.8 tonnes (triple), 26.5 tonnes (twin)
- **Max Range**: 25,700 m (28,100 yds) @ 40°
- **Notable**: Most produced German naval gun. Versatile and reliable. Used as main battery on light cruisers, secondary on capital ships.
- **Source**: NavWeaps WNGER_59-55_skc28

- **Notable**: German destroyer guns were surface-only unlike Allied DP guns. Fast firing. Remained in Norwegian coastal defense until 2003!
