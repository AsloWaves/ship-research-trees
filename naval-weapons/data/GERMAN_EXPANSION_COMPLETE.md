## Historical Research Highlights

### **Major German Naval Guns**

1. **40.6cm/50 SK C/34** (Plan Z H-class)
   - Never built, intended for H-44 super-battleships
   - Larger than Bismarck's 38cm guns

2. **38cm/52 SK C/34** (Bismarck, Tirpitz)
   - Most famous German battleship gun
   - 820 m/s muzzle velocity, 800kg AP shell
   - 3 RPM combat-proven rate

3. **28cm/54.5 SK C/34** (Scharnhorst, Gneisenau)
   - Longest-range hit in naval history: 26,465 yards (HMS Glorious)
   - 300kg shells at 960 m/s

4. **28cm/52 SK C/28** (Deutschland-class pocket battleships)
   - "Pocket battleship" main armament
   - Diesel propulsion for extended range

5. **20.3cm/60 SK C/34** (Admiral Hipper heavy cruisers)
   - Only German 8" gun design
   - 925 m/s muzzle velocity (very high)

6. **15cm/55 SK C/28** (Most common German naval gun)
   - Used on all cruisers and as secondary armament
   - Bismarck had 12× as secondary battery

7. **10.5cm/65 SK C/33** (Primary dual-purpose gun)
   - Based on famous land-based FlaK 38/39 "88"
   - 85° elevation, 15-18 RPM

8. **8.8cm/76 SK C/35** (Naval "88" AA gun)
   - Famous "Acht-Acht" adapted for naval use
   - Quad mounts common, 20+ RPM per barrel

## German Naming Conventions

### **Gun Designations**
- **SK** = SchiffsKanone (Ship's Cannon)
- **C/XX** = Construction year (e.g., C/34 = 1934)
- **L/XX** = Länge (Length in calibers)
- **Flak** = Flugzeugabwehrkanone (Anti-Aircraft Cannon)

### **Ammunition Designations**
- **P.Spr.Gr.** = Panzersprenggranate (Armor-Piercing HE)
- **Spr.Gr.** = Sprenggranate (High-Explosive)
- **Bdz** = mit Bodenzünder (with Base Fuze)
- **Kz** = Kopfzünder (Nose Fuze)
- **Zt.Z.** = Zeitzünder (Time Fuze)
- **Üb.Gr.** = Übungsgranate (Practice Shell)
- **Br.Gr.** = Brisanzgranate (Armor-Piercing, no cap)
- **Pzgr.** = Panzergranate (Armor-Piercing)

### **Turret Designations**
- **Einzellafette** = Single mount
- **Doppellafette** = Twin mount
- **Drillingsafette** = Triple mount
- **Vierlingsafette** = Quad mount
