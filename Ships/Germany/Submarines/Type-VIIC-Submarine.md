Type VIIC ALONE comprised 81-84% of all Type VII boats! **"Workhorse" production efficiency**: +35% production efficiency special ability reflects unprecedented mass production. Standardized design enabled multiple shipyards to build simultaneously. Modular construction techniques. Prefabricated sections. Assembly-line methods. Average build time: 72 days (faster than Allied escorts!). Peak production: 30 boats/month (1943)! **Battle of Atlantic backbone**: Type VIIC formed core of U-boat force 1941-1945. Operating from French bases (Brest, Lorient, St. Nazaire, La Rochelle, Bordeaux) after France fell (June 1940). Attacked Allied convoys bringing supplies from North America to Britain. Nearly starved Britain into surrender 1942-1943. **Wolf pack tactics** (Rudeltaktik): Type VIIC boats operated in coordinated groups (8-20 boats). Shore command (BdU - Befehlshaber der U-Boote) coordinated attacks via radio. One boat found convoy → called pack → simultaneous attack from multiple directions → overwhelmed escorts. Devastating 1940-1943 before Allies cracked Enigma codes and deployed escort carriers. **Optimized design**: 769 tons surfaced (slightly larger than VIIB 753 tons). 220.2 ft length. 8,500 nm range enabled mid-Atlantic operations. 5× torpedo tubes, 14 torpedoes. 8.8cm deck gun (removed late-war for extra AA armament as Allied air power increased). **AA armament evolution**: Early-war: 1× 2cm Flak.
