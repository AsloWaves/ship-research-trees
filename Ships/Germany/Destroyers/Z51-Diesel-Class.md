Could operate across Atlantic without tanker support. **Design philosophy**: Diesel engines offered extended range, reduced fuel consumption, simpler machinery, fewer crew requirements. Eliminated boilers, funnels simplified, machinery spaces smaller. **Technical challenges**: Diesel technology unproven at destroyer power levels. Vibration concerns with high-speed diesels. Weight penalties. Maintenance complexity. Reliability unproven in combat conditions. Steam turbines remained preferred technology. **Historical significance**: First serious German attempt at diesel destroyer - decades ahead of time (diesel destroyers not viable until 1960s+). Shows German willingness to experiment with radical technologies despite war pressures. **Why cancelled**: War ended before diesel technology proven, resources prioritized for operational ships, experimental risk too high. Diesel destroyer concept abandoned postwar. **Legacy**: Diesel destroyer concept ahead of its time - not viable until modern era (gas turbines eventually replaced steam, but diesel never succeeded for large warships). Shows limits of wartime innovation when basic technology unproven.
