**REVOLUTIONARY 15cm Gun Introduction** - 2 ships built for Russia, seized by Germany (Aug 1914): Pillau, Elbing. **Game-changer**: First German light cruisers with 15cm (5.9-inch) SK L/45 guns, **+30% firepower** vs. 10.5cm. 15cm became **German naval standard through WWII**. 4,390 tons, 8× 15cm guns, 29.4 knots (fastest trial). First gun shields (50mm). **SMS Elbing**: Sunk at Battle of Jutland (1 Jun 1916, 4 killed, 438 survivors). **SMS Pillau**: Transferred to Italy 1920 (war prize), served as Bari (1920-1943), scrapped 1943.

**Key Features:** FIRST 15cm guns (German standard through WWII) | +30% firepower vs. 10.5cm | Elbing: Sunk at Jutland | Pillau: Italian war prize (Bari, 1920-1943)

**Ships:** SMS Pillau (transferred Italy 1920 as Bari, scrapped 1943), SMS Elbing (collision damage at Jutland, scuttled 1 Jun 1916, 4 killed, 438 survivors)

**Service:** WWI High Seas Fleet (1914-1918). Originally built for Russian Navy (Admiral Nevelskoy, Admiral Makarov), seized by Germany Aug 1914. Battle of Jutland (31 May-1 Jun 1916): Elbing rammed by German battleship Posen, scuttled 1 Jun 1916 (4 killed, 438 survivors). Pillau: High Seas Fleet service, surrendered 1918, transferred Italy 1920 (war prize), served Italian Navy as Bari (1920-1943), scuttled 1943. **Revolutionary significance**: 15cm gun introduction changed German light cruiser doctrine forever.
