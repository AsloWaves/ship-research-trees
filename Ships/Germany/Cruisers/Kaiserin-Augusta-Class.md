**Germany's Largest Protected Cruiser (Single Ship)** - Commissioned 1892, decommissioned 1919 (27 years service, longest-serving German cruiser). 6,318 tons, 12× 15cm guns, 18.5 knots. Largest German protected cruiser, demonstrated superiority of uniform 15cm armament over mixed calibers. Survived WWI (decommissioned 1914 due to obsolescence, converted to depot ship). Unique single-ship design, bridged gap between early protected cruisers and Victoria Louise class.

**Key Features:** Largest German protected cruiser (6,318 tons) | 12× 15cm guns (heavy armament) | 27 years service (1892-1919) | Survived WWI

**Ships:** SMS Kaiserin Augusta (single ship)

**Service:** Peacetime overseas service (1892-1914): Mediterranean, East Asia, training. WWI: Decommissioned 1914 due to obsolescence, converted to depot ship Kiel. Survived WWI, used as accommodation ship 1914-1919. Scrapped 1919. Longest-serving German cruiser (27 years), demonstrated transition from small protected cruisers to modern light cruisers. Uniform 15cm armament influenced later German cruiser design.
