**First German Heavy Cruiser Lost WWII (Admiral Hipper-Class Ship 2)** - Commissioned 1939, second Admiral Hipper-class ship. 14,050 tons, 8× 20.3cm guns, 32 knots. Named after famous armored cruiser SMS Blücher (sunk Dogger Bank 1915). **Operation Weserübung** (Norway invasion, 9 Apr 1940): Leading German naval force to Oslo, **Drøbak Narrows disaster** - torpedoed by Norwegian coastal fortress Oscarsborg (2 torpedoes hit), heavy gunfire (28cm guns), crippled and sinking, capsized and sank (830 killed, 770 survivors). **First German heavy cruiser lost WWII**. Shortest service life of Admiral Hipper class (8 months). Prevented German seizure of Oslo on first day, Norwegian government escaped.

**Key Features:** First German heavy cruiser lost WWII (9 Apr 1940) | Drøbak Narrows disaster (830 killed) | Shortest service life (8 months) | Operation Weserübung

**Ships:** Blücher (single ship, second Admiral Hipper-class, sunk 9 Apr 1940, 830 killed, wreck remains in Oslo Fjord)

**Service:** WWII operations (1939-1940). Commissioned 20 Sep 1939, worked up in Baltic. Operation Weserübung (Norway invasion, 9 Apr 1940): Flagship of German naval force to Oslo, carried 1,000+ troops, Gestapo agents, administrative staff. **Drøbak Narrows** (0430 hrs, 9 Apr 1940): Norwegian coastal fortress Oscarsborg torpedoed (2× 45cm torpedoes hit, massive damage), heavy gunfire (2× 28cm + 15cm guns), crippled, fires uncontrolled, ammunition explosions, capsized 0730 hrs, sank. 830 killed (sailors + troops), 770 survivors. Prevented German seizure of Oslo on first day, Norwegian King and government escaped to London. Wreck remains in Oslo Fjord, designated war grave.
