**First German Heavy Cruiser Class (Lead Ship)** - Commissioned 1939, lead ship of Germany's only heavy cruiser class. 14,050 tons, 8× 20.3cm (8-inch) guns in twin turrets, 32.5 knots. First German cruiser with 8-inch guns and advanced fire control radar. Atlantic operations (1940-1942): Commerce raiding, convoy attacks. **Battle of Barents Sea** (31 Dec 1942): Failed to destroy Convoy JW 51B, damaged by British destroyers, strategic failure. Limited operations 1943-1945 due to fuel shortages. Scuttled at Kiel (3 May 1945) to avoid Soviet capture. Raised 1945-1948, scrapped.

**Key Features:** First German heavy cruiser (8× 20.3cm guns) | Largest German cruiser (14,050 tons) | Battle of Barents Sea (31 Dec 1942) | Scuttled Kiel 3 May 1945

**Ships:** Admiral Hipper (single ship, lead of 5-ship class, scuttled 3 May 1945, scrapped 1948-1952)

**Service:** WWII operations (1939-1945). Atlantic operations (1940-1942): Norway invasion (Operation Weserübung, Apr 1940), commerce raiding (sank 12 merchant ships, 62,662 GRT), convoy attacks. Battle of Barents Sea (31 Dec 1942): Attack on Convoy JW 51B, damaged by British destroyers Onslow and Obedient, withdrew without destroying convoy, Hitler's fury led to plan to scrap entire surface fleet. Limited operations 1943-1945 due to fuel shortages and Allied air superiority. Scuttled at Kiel (3 May 1945) to avoid Soviet capture. Raised 1945-1948, scrapped 1948-1952.
