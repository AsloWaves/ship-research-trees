**Fast S-boat Tenders** - Carl Peters-class were first purpose-built large fast S-boat tenders. 2 built 1938-1940 at AG Neptun, Rostock.

**Key Specs:** 3,600 tons | 114m | 23 kn | 225 crew | 12,000nm range | 4× 10.5cm SK C/32

**Innovation:** First purpose-built large-scale tenders specifically for Schnellboot flotilla support. At 23 knots, significantly faster than earlier tenders (Tsingtau/Tanga: 17.5 kn). Exceptional 12,000nm range enabled extended deployments. Combined accommodation (barracks for S-boat crews), supply depot (fuel, torpedoes, mines, ammunition), medical facilities (hospital services), and maintenance support in single platform.

**Design:** Built AG Neptun, Rostock. Ordered 1938. Carl Peters launched 13 Apr 1939, commissioned 6 Jan 1940. Adolf Lüderitz launched 20 Feb 1939, commissioned 11 Jun 1940. 2× MAN four-stroke diesels, 12,400hp. 4× 10.5cm naval guns, multiple AA weapons. Named after colonial figures: Carl Peters (East Africa administrator), Adolf Lüderitz (South West Africa businessman).

**Service:** Carl Peters: Operation Weserübung Norway Apr 1940 (Bergen occupation, hit by Norwegian torpedo Storm—failed to detonate), 1st S-Flotilla North Sea/English Channel (1940-1941), Baltic Sea May 1941 (Operation Barbarossa), 6th/8th S-Flotilla Norway (1942), training role (1943). Struck mine Geltinger Bay 10 May 1945, sank. Adolf Lüderitz: 3rd S-Flotilla Netherlands/English Channel (1940-1941), 8th S-Flotilla Norway Nov 1941 (attacked by Norwegian sub Uredd 17 Jan 1943, escaped), 6th S-Flotilla (1942). Damaged air raid Jøssingfjord 14 Apr 1945. Captured Stavanger 8 May 1945, used German Mine Clearance Service, transferred Soviet Union 1946 as Pajserd, served until 1964.
