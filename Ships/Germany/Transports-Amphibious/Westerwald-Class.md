**Ammunition Transports** - Westerwald-class (Type 760) were specialized ammunition/material transports for warships at sea. 2 ships 1967-2010.

**Key Specs:** 4,032 tons | 105.3m × 14.0m × 3.7m | 17 kn | 58 crew | 1,080 tons cargo

**Innovation:** Specialized replenishment ships designed specifically for transporting ammunition and material to warships at sea. Two diesel engines producing 4,200 kW total driving twin propellers. Equipped with specialized cargo handling: two 3-ton onboard cranes, two 2.5-ton derricks, ten loading hatches, two supply stations. Armament for self-defense: 4× 40mm AA guns. Could deliver up to 1,080 tons of munitions and supplies to fleet units during underway replenishment operations.

**Design:** Ordered from Orenstein & Koppel shipyard Lübeck end of 1964 as Type 706 ships (later redesignated Type 760). Two ships named after German mountain ranges: Westerwald (A1435) and Odenwald (A1436). Commissioned 1967. Relatively small crew of 58 personnel operated ships and cargo handling equipment.

**Service:** Operated throughout Cold War and post-reunification era supporting Bundesmarine/German Navy fleet operations in Baltic and North Sea. Primary role: transporting ammunition, torpedoes, missiles, and material supplies from shore depots to operational warships at sea or in forward bases. Both ships served 43 years 1967-2010. Decommissioned 2010. Replaced by larger Berlin-class (Type 702) combat support ships.
