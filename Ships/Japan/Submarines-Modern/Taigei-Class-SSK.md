Better hydrodynamics, reduced acoustic signature. 6 torpedo tubes, 30 weapons. 650+ meter test depth. Crew 70 (vs Soryu's 65 - slightly larger despite automation). All active as of 2025. Represent latest evolution of Japanese submarine technology. Will replace remaining Oyashio-class boats. Expected total: 7-10 boats over 2020s-2030s.

**All Lithium-Ion from Start:**

**Lesson from Soryu:**
- Soryu: SS-501 to SS-510 had AIP (10 boats)
- Soryu: SS-511 to SS-512 had lithium-ion (2 boats)
- **Taigei: All lithium-ion** (no AIP variants)

**Why No AIP:**
- JMSDF evaluated both technologies in Soryu program
- Conclusion: **Lithium-ion better for Japanese needs**
  1. **Mission profile:** Japan-centric operations, not far from bases
  2. **Performance:** Li-ion provides better speed/maneuverability
  3. **Maintenance:** Simpler than AIP (no Stirling engines, no LOX)
  4. **Flexibility:** Faster recharge, better tactical options
  5. **Space:** No AIP = more room for batteries, sensors, weapons

**AIP Advantage Lost:**
- AIP: 2+ weeks submerged at 4-6 knots (very slow but long endurance)
- Li-ion: Shorter endurance than AIP (but longer than lead-acid), but faster speeds

**JMSDF Decision:**
- Speed/performance > maximum endurance
- Taigei all Li-ion = confidence in technology

**6,000 shp Electric Motor:**

**Major Improvement:**
- Soryu: 2,900 shp electric motor
- **Taigei: 6,000 shp** (~2× more powerful!)

**Advantages:**
1. **Higher submerged speed:** 20+ knots sustained longer
2. **Better acceleration:** Faster sprint capability
3. **Tactical flexibility:** Quick position changes
4. **Pursuit/evasion:** Chase targets or escape threats
5. **Battery efficiency:** Better power management

**Enabled by Lithium-Ion:**
- Lead-acid batteries: Can't deliver high power for long
- **Li-ion: High discharge rates sustained**
- More powerful motor takes advantage of Li-ion capability

**Result:**
- Taigei among fastest conventional submarines
- Sprint capability rivals some nuclear submarines (briefly)
