**Coastal Defenders** - RO-100-class were small coastal defense submarines. 18 boats built 1942-1944. Very small (601/782 tons), four torpedoes with no reloads. 14 knots surfaced, 8 submerged. Mass-produced for homeland defense but many sent to distant waters. 13 lost in combat (72% loss rate). Showed limitations of coastal subs in Pacific - too small, too slow, too limited. Five survived to surrender.

**Key Specs:** 601/782 tons | 197ft | 14.2/8 kn | 4× 533mm tubes (4 torpedoes, no reloads) | 18 boats

**Innovation:** Mass-produced coastal defense submarines. Very small for economy and rapid construction. Four bow torpedo tubes but only four torpedoes carried (no reloads) - one-shot design. Single 25mm AA gun. Diesel-electric propulsion. 14 knots surfaced (slow even for coastal subs), 8 knots submerged. Limited range (3,500 nm). Designed for coastal defense but many sent to distant theaters where inadequate. 18 built rapidly 1942-1944. 13 lost (72%) showed unsuitable for Pacific warfare. Too small to fight large warships, too slow to escape, too limited for long patrols.

**Design:** Four bow torpedo tubes. Four torpedoes total - no reloads (severe limitation). Single 25mm AA gun on deck. Very small (197 ft - smaller than most destroyers). Small crew (38). Limited endurance. Diesel-electric propulsion (slow - 14 knots surfaced). Pressure hull for ~250 feet depth. Simple construction for mass production. No luxuries - cramped, basic. Designed for short-range coastal patrol, not Pacific operations. Misused by sending to distant waters.

**USS Batfish's Rampage (February 1945):**
*Three RO-class in four days*

**Background:**
- **USS Batfish (SS-310)** on patrol near Philippines
- February 10-13, 1945
- Detected multiple RO-class submarines

**February 10, 1945 - RO-113:**
- USS Batfish detected RO-113 surfaced
- Fired spread of torpedoes
- **Hit and sank RO-113**
- All hands lost

**February 11, 1945 - RO-112:**
- Next night, detected another submarine
- RO-112 surfaced (charging batteries)
- USS Batfish attacked
- **Sank RO-112**
- All hands lost

**February 13, 1945 - RO-115:**
- Third submarine detected
- RO-115 also surfaced
- USS Batfish attacked
- **Sank RO-115**
- All hands lost

**Record:**
- **3 submarines sunk in 4 days**
- Only submarine to sink 3 enemy subs
- Record never broken
- All three victims RO-100-class

**Why RO-Class So Vulnerable:**
1. **Slow:** 14 knots surfaced - couldn't escape
2. **Loud:** Diesel engines noisy
3. **Surfaced frequently:** Limited battery capacity
4. **No radar:** Couldn't detect Batfish
5. **Predictable:** Similar patrol patterns
6. **Small:** Limited sensors, equipment

**Loss Analysis:**

**13 of 18 lost (72% loss rate):**
**Causes:**
- ASW surface ships: ~8 boats
- Submarines (USS Batfish): 3 boats
- Aircraft: ~2 boats

**Why Such High Losses:**
1. **Misused:** Coastal subs sent to distant waters
2. **Slow:** Couldn't evade hunters
3. **Limited weapons:** 4 torpedoes, no reloads
4. **Small:** Limited survivability
5. **Allied ASW:** Improving rapidly 1944-1945
6. **Radar:** US radar detected surfaced subs easily

**Tactical Limitations:**

**Design Intent:**
- Coastal defense
- Short patrols (few days)
- Near bases (quick return)
- Against invasion forces

**Actual Use:**
- Long-range missions
- Week+ patrols
- Far from bases
- Against prepared ASW forces

**Result:** Square peg, round hole - wrong tool for wrong job

**RO-100: Worst survival rate**

**Why Only 4 Torpedoes:**
**Design Compromise:**
- Small hull (601 tons)
- Limited space
- Four tubes, four torpedoes
- **No reload capacity**
- After four shots, defenseless

**Problem:**
- One attack, then return to base
- No second chances
- If miss, mission failed
- Severely limited effectiveness

**Mass Production:**
**Why 18 Built:**
1. **Numbers needed:** Sub shortage acute
2. **Quick construction:** 8-10 months
3. **Economical:** Small, simple, cheap
4. **Coastal defense:** Planned invasion expected
5. **Resources:** Steel/labor limited

**Result:**
- 18 boats delivered quickly
- But: Wrong boats for Pacific war
- Most lost before useful

**Survivors (5 boats):**
**Why Some Survived:**
1. **Luck:** Avoided hunters
2. **Timing:** Commissioned late (less exposure)
3. **Location:** Stayed closer to Japan
4. **War ended:** Before more losses

**All scrapped 1948** - no value post-war

**Legacy:** RO-100-class represented mass-produced coastal defense submarines. 18 boats built rapidly 1942-1944. Small (601 tons), slow (14 knots), limited (4 torpedoes, no reloads). Designed for coastal defense but many sent to distant Pacific operations where inadequate. 72% loss rate (13 of 18) showed fundamental mismatch. USS Batfish sank three in four days (February 1945) - exposed vulnerability. Five survived but scrapped 1948. Showed wrong submarines for wrong war. Coastal defense design useless in vast Pacific. Names: RO-101 through RO-118 (number designations).

**Historical Significance:** RO-100-class epitomized Japan's submarine crisis. Built cheap coastal subs (4 torpedoes, no reloads!) when needed ocean-going fleet subs. 18 boats mass-produced but 72% lost (13 of 18). USS Batfish's three kills in four days (February 1945) exposed fundamental weakness - slow, limited, vulnerable. Designed for coastal defense of Japan but sent to Philippines, Carolines, distant waters where completely inadequate. Four torpedoes with no reloads meant one attack then return - absurd for Pacific distances. Showed desperation - building wrong submarines because quick and cheap, not because effective. Compare to US fleet subs (1,500+ tons, 24 torpedoes) - RO-100 class hopelessly outmatched. Five survivors scrapped 1948. Represented quantity over quality - failed strategy when quality matters.
