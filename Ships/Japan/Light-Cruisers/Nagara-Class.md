**1920s Destroyer Leaders** - Nagara-class were improved Kuma-class light cruisers. 6 ships built 1922-1923. Seven 5.5-inch guns + eight 24-inch torpedoes. High speed (36 knots). All six lost in combat 1942-1945 (100% loss rate). Participated extensively in every major campaign from Pearl Harbor to Philippines.

**Key Specs:** 5,170/5,832 tons | 535ft | 36 kn | 7× 140mm + 8× 610mm Type 93 torpedoes | 63mm belt | 6 ships

**Innovation:** Improved Kuma-class with better seakeeping and slightly larger displacement. Seven single 5.5-inch guns. Eight Type 93 Long Lance torpedoes (after modernization). High speed (36 knots) for destroyer flotilla leader role. Good range (5,000 nm). All six saw extensive WWII service. 100% combat loss rate. Last Japanese cruisers with 5.5-inch main guns.

**Design:** Seven single 5.5-inch guns (four forward, three aft - all centerline). Two quad 24-inch torpedo launchers. High speed (36 knots). Light armor (2.5-inch belt). One floatplane with catapult. Mine-laying capability (48 mines). Long, sleek hull for speed. Destroyer flotilla leader concept - command destroyer squadrons.

**Ships (6 built - all lost):**
1. **Nagara** - Sunk August 7, 1944 (submarine USS Croaker)
2. **Isuzu** - Sunk April 7, 1945 (submarine USS Gabilan and Charr)
3. **Natori** - Sunk August 18, 1944 (submarine USS Hardhead)
4. **Yura** - Sunk October 25, 1942 (air attack, Guadalcanal)
5. **Kinu** - Sunk October 26, 1944 (air attack, Philippines)
6. **Abukuma** - Sunk October 27, 1944 (air attack, Philippines)

**Construction:**
- **Nagara:** Sasebo Naval Arsenal, laid down September 9, 1920, launched April 25, 1921, commissioned April 21, 1922
- **Isuzu:** Uraga Dock Company, laid down August 10, 1920, launched October 29, 1921, commissioned August 15, 1923
- **Natori:** Mitsubishi Nagasaki, laid down November 18, 1920, launched February 16, 1922, commissioned September 15, 1922
- **Yura:** Sasebo Naval Arsenal, laid down May 21, 1921, launched February 15, 1922, commissioned March 20, 1923
- **Kinu:** Kawasaki Kobe, laid down January 17, 1921, launched May 29, 1922, commissioned November 10, 1922
- **Abukuma:** Uraga Dock Company, laid down December 8, 1921, launched March 16, 1923, commissioned May 26, 1925

**Service - Major Operations:**

**Pearl Harbor Support (December 1941):**
- **Nagara:** Supported carrier striking force, Wake Island invasion
- Multiple Nagara-class participated in Philippines and Dutch East Indies invasions

**Battle of Midway (June 4-5, 1942) - Nagara:**
- Flagship of DesRon 10 (destroyer squadron 10)
- Screened carriers Hiryu and Soryu
- Witnessed sinking of Hiryu (last carrier sunk)
- Rescued survivors from Hiryu
- Retreated with surviving forces

**Guadalcanal Campaign:**

**Battle of Savo Island (August 9, 1942) - Yura:**
- Part of screening force
- Japanese victory - 4 Allied cruisers sunk
- Yura undamaged

**October 1942 - Yura Lost:**
- **October 25, 1942:** Transporting troops/supplies to Guadalcanal
- Attacked by US carrier aircraft from USS Hornet
- Hit by multiple bombs
- Dead in water, fires uncontrollable
- Scuttled by destroyer torpedoes
- **138 killed**
- First Nagara-class lost

**Battle of Leyte Gulf (October 23-26, 1944):**
*Three Nagara-class lost in 3 days*

**Background:**
- Multiple Nagara-class participated in various forces
- Kinu and Abukuma in different squadrons

**October 26, 1944 - Kinu:**
- Part of transport/support force
- Attacked by US carrier aircraft in Panay region
- Hit by bombs and rockets
- **Capsized and sank**
- **Heavy casualties**

**October 27, 1944 - Abukuma:**
- Damaged October 25 in Surigao Strait (torpedo hit)
- Limping back to Manila
- **October 27:** Attacked again by aircraft
- Hit by additional bombs
- **Sank in shallow water**
- **240 killed**

**Submarine Losses:**
**Three sunk by US submarines:**

**August 7, 1944 - Nagara:**
- Transiting Formosa Strait
- **USS Croaker (SS-246)** torpedo attack
- Multiple torpedo hits
- **Sank quickly**
- **525 killed, 232 survivors**

**August 18, 1944 - Natori:**
- Off Luzon, Philippines
- **USS Hardhead (SS-365)** torpedo attack
- Four torpedoes fired, at least two hit
- **Sank rapidly**
- **300 killed**

**April 7, 1945 - Isuzu:**
*Last Nagara-class lost*
- Departing Sumbawa, Dutch East Indies
- Attacked by **USS Gabilan (SS-252) and USS Charr (SS-328)**
- Coordinated attack - 5 torpedoes hit
- **Capsized and sank**
- **190 killed**
- Last surviving Nagara-class

**Modernization (1930s):**
- Torpedo tubes upgraded to Type 93 Long Lance
- AA armament increased (25mm guns added)
- Aircraft catapult improved
- Radar added (late war)
- Fire control upgraded

**Notable Ships:**

- Flagship at Midway (DesRon 10)
- Rescued Hiryu survivors
- Survived until August 1944
- Sunk by USS Croaker

**Isuzu:**
- Last survivor (until April 1945)

- Increased AA: 41× 25mm guns

**Yura:**
- First loss (October 1942)

**Kinu and Abukuma:**
- Both lost Leyte Gulf (October 1944)

**Legacy:** Nagara-class represented mature 1920s destroyer leader design. All six participated extensively in WWII - Pearl Harbor support, Midway, Guadalcanal, Philippines. 100% loss rate (all six sunk) demonstrated heavy attrition. Three lost to submarines (50%), three to aircraft (50%) - showed dual threat that destroyed IJN. Nagara at Midway witnessed carrier battle as flagship. Isuzu last survivor (April 1945) showed some lasted to war's end but none survived. Names: Rivers in Japan.

**Historical Significance:** Nagara-class epitomized IJN destroyer flotilla leader concept. Six ships provided extensive service across all campaigns. 100% loss rate showed no safe haven by 1944-1945. Even distribution of losses (50% submarine, 50% air) demonstrated dual threat that overwhelmed IJN. Nagara's Midway flagship role witnessed turning point of war. Three ships lost in quick succession at Leyte Gulf (October 1944) showed desperation of Philippines defense. Isuzu's survival to April 1945 but ultimate loss showed that even last survivors eventually fell. Represented 1920s design serving in 1940s war - obsolete but fought to end.
