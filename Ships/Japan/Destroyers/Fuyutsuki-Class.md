**Refined AA Destroyers** - Fuyutsuki-class were improved Akizuki-class AA destroyers. 12 ships commissioned 1944-1945. Eight 100mm DP guns + 40-52 light AA. 33 knots. Refined design with better stability, more AA. Most commissioned too late for significant combat (1945). 6 lost, 6 survived (50% survival rate). Last IJN destroyers built in quantity. Represented peak Japanese AA destroyer design but too late to matter.

**Key Specs:** 2,701/3,759 tons | 440ft | 33 kn | 8× 100mm DP + 40-52× 25mm AA + 4× 610mm torpedoes | Unarmored | 12 ships

**Innovation:** Improved Akizuki-class design. Better stability than Akizuki (less top-heavy). Eight 100mm/65 Type 98 DP guns (excellent AA capability). Up to 52× 25mm light AA. Four torpedoes (vs eight on Akizuki - reduced for stability). 72 depth charges (excellent ASW). 33 knots (vs 33 for Akizuki). Long range (8,000 nm). Commissioned late war (1944-1945) - most saw minimal combat. 50% survival rate good for late-war ships. Last AA destroyers built - peak design but timing terrible.

**Design:** Four twin 100mm DP turrets (two forward superfiring, two aft superfiring). Heavy light AA (up to 52× 25mm). One quad torpedo launcher amidships (reduced from Akizuki's two). 72 depth charges (double throwers). Improved stability over Akizuki (less topweight). Good range (8,000 nm).
