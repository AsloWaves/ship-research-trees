**Pre-War Excellence** - Asashio-class were improved Shiratsuyu-class destroyers. 10 ships built 1937-1938. Six 5-inch guns in three twin turrets + eight 24-inch torpedoes. All 10 lost in combat 1942-1943 (100% loss rate). Set template for subsequent Kagero and Yugumo classes. Heavy Guadalcanal losses.

**Key Specs:** 1,961/2,370 tons | 388ft | 35 kn | 6× 127mm + 8× 610mm Type 93 torpedoes | Unarmored | 10 ships

**Innovation:** Three twin 5-inch turrets (improved over Shiratsuyu's single mounts). Better stability. Larger displacement (1,961 tons). Set standard for future Special Type destroyers. Template directly used by Kagero-class. Good balance of guns and torpedoes. All 10 saw extensive Guadalcanal combat. 100% loss rate in just 18 months (June 1942-December 1943).

**Design:** Three twin 5-inch turrets (two forward superfiring, one aft). Two quad 24-inch torpedo launchers amidships. High speed (35 knots). Good range (5,000 nm). Eight Type 93 Long Lance torpedoes. Light AA (increased during war). Depth charges for ASW. Refined destroyer design - influenced all subsequent IJN destroyers.

**Ships (10 built - all lost):**
1. **Asashio** - Sunk March 4, 1943 (air attack, Bismarck Sea)
2. **Oshio** - Sunk May 8, 1943 (submarine USS Triton)
3. **Michishio** - Sunk October 25, 1944 (surface action, Surigao Strait)
4. **Arashio** - Sunk March 3, 1943 (air attack, Bismarck Sea)
5. **Asagumo** - Sunk October 25, 1944 (surface action, Surigao Strait)
6. **Yamagumo** - Sunk June 25, 1942 (submarine USS Nautilus)
7. **Minegumo** - Sunk March 6, 1943 (air attack, Bismarck Sea)
8. **Natsugumo** - Sunk February 2, 1943 (surface action, Rennell Island)
9. **Arare** - Sunk July 5, 1942 (air attack, Aleutians)
10. **Kasumi** - Sunk April 7, 1945 (air attack, Operation Ten-Go)

**Construction:**
- Builders: Uraga, Maizuru, Sasebo, Fujinagata
- Laid down: 1935-1937
- Commissioned: 1937-1938

**Major Battles:**

**Battle of Java Sea (February 27, 1942):**
- Several Asashio-class participated
- Torpedo attacks against Allied cruisers
- Contributed to sinking HMS Exeter and destroyers

**Battle of Midway (June 4-6, 1942) - Yamagumo:**
- Escorted battleship/carrier force
- Yamagumo torpedoed and sank USS Hammann (destroyer) near Yorktown wreck
- **June 25, 1942:** Yamagumo sunk by USS Nautilus (same sub that damaged Soryu)

**Aleutians Campaign - Arare:**
- Part of Northern Force supporting Aleutians invasion
- **July 5, 1942:** Attacked by B-17 bombers off Kiska
- Hit by bombs, sank
- Early loss in Aleutian campaign

**Battle of Rennell Island (January 29-30, 1943) - Natsugumo:**
- Night torpedo attack by Japanese aircraft on US task force
- Natsugumo part of screening force
- Damaged by US aircraft
- **February 2, 1943:** Sank from accumulated damage

**Battle of Bismarck Sea (March 2-3, 1943):**
*Disaster - 3 Asashio-class sunk in two days*

**Background:**
- Escorting troop convoy to New Guinea
- 8 transports, 8 destroyers (including 3 Asashio-class)
- Attacked by 340+ Allied aircraft (US/Australian)

**March 3-4, 1943:**
- **Arashio:** Hit by bombs, sank March 3
- **Asashio:** Hit by bombs and torpedoes, sank March 4
- **Minegumo:** Hit by bombs, sank March 6
- Entire convoy destroyed
- Worst single disaster for Asashio-class

**Battle of Surigao Strait (October 25, 1944) - Michishio, Asagumo:**
*Last battleship action*

**Background:**
- Part of Admiral Nishimura's Southern Force
- Mission: Break through Surigao Strait to Leyte Gulf
- Force: Battleships Yamashiro and Fuso, cruiser Mogami, 4 destroyers

**October 25, 1944 (~3:00 AM):**
- Ran into US ambush - 6 battleships, 8 cruisers, 28 destroyers
- Torpedo attacks from US destroyers
- **Michishio:** Hit by torpedoes, exploded and sank (~3:10 AM)
- **Asagumo:** Damaged, withdrew, attacked by US cruisers
- **Asagumo:** Sank later from accumulated damage and gunfire

**Operation Ten-Go (April 7, 1945) - Kasumi:**
*Yamato's suicide mission*

**Background:**
- Escorted Yamato to Okinawa (suicide mission)
- Force: Yamato, Yahagi, 8 destroyers

**April 7, 1945:**
- Attacked by 400+ US carrier aircraft
- Kasumi damaged early in attacks
- Hit by bombs and rockets
- Dead in water
- Yamato sank 2:23 PM
- **Kasumi:** Scuttled by friendly forces (couldn't be towed)
- **Last Asashio-class lost**

**Loss Statistics:**
- **10 built, 10 lost (100% loss rate)**
- **Causes:**
  - Air attack: 5 ships (50%)
  - Submarine: 1 ship (10%)
  - Surface action: 2 ships (20%)
  - Scuttled after damage: 1 ship (10%)
  - Surface/air combined: 1 ship (10%)
- **Loss period:** June 1942-April 1945 (35 months)

1. **Extensive combat employment:** All 10 saw heavy action
2. **Guadalcanal/Solomons:** Multiple ships lost in campaigns
3. **Bismarck Sea disaster:** 3 ships lost in 2 days
4. **No safe haven:** Constant combat until war's end
5. **No luck:** Unlike Shigure or Yukikaze, none survived

**Tactical Employment:**
- **Fleet screening:** High-speed escorts for carriers/battleships
- **Tokyo Express:** Fast supply runs to Guadalcanal
- **Night actions:** Torpedo attacks on US forces
- **Convoy escort:** Protecting transports
- **ASW:** Depth charge attacks on submarines

**Design Legacy:**
- **Template for Kagero:** Directly inspired subsequent class
- **Twin turrets:** Became standard for IJN destroyers
- **Displacement:** 1,961 tons became baseline
- **Balance:** Guns + torpedoes + speed formula perfected
- **No survivors:** But design lived on in successor classes

**Notable Ships:**

**Yamagumo:**
- Sank USS Hammann at Midway (June 6, 1942)
- Itself sunk by USS Nautilus (June 25, 1942)
- Short but eventful service

**Kasumi:**
- Longest survivor (until April 1945)
- Operation Ten-Go participant
- Last Asashio-class lost
- Scuttled after Yamato sunk

**Bismarck Sea Trio (Asashio, Arashio, Minegumo):**
- All three sunk March 2-6, 1943
- Escorting doomed convoy
- Overwhelmed by Allied air power
- Worst single loss for class

**Legacy:** Asashio-class represented refined pre-war destroyer design. Template for Kagero and Yugumo classes. All 10 lost in combat (100% casualty rate) - tied with Shiratsuyu for highest destroyer class loss rate. Bismarck Sea disaster (3 ships in 2 days) showed vulnerability to air attack. Surigao Strait (2 ships) showed courage in hopeless battles. Kasumi's service until April 1945 showed dedication - fought to bitter end with Yamato. Design excellence but 100% attrition. Names: Natural phenomena (morning tide, waves, storms).

**Historical Significance:** Asashio-class epitomized IJN Special Type destroyer evolution. Improved Shiratsuyu design, inspired Kagero. 100% loss rate (all 10 sunk) demonstrated unsustainable destroyer attrition. Bismarck Sea disaster single worst day - 3 ships lost. Participated in every major campaign - Java Sea, Midway, Guadalcanal, Solomons, Philippines, Okinawa. Kasumi at Operation Ten-Go showed fighting spirit - escorted Yamato to death. Design successful but numbers insufficient - quality couldn't overcome US quantity and air power. Represented peak pre-war design entering war that destroyed them all.
