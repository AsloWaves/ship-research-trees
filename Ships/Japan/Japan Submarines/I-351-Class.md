**Underwater Transports** - I-351-class were Japanese transport submarines. 3 boats commissioned 1944-1945. Purpose-built cargo submarines - 300 tons cargo capacity, could carry 50-60 troops. No torpedoes (pure transport). Large (3,512/4,290 tons), slow (15.8/6.3 knots). Desperate late-war design for supplying isolated garrisons when surface shipping impossible. All three lost 1945 (100% loss rate). I-351 sunk by USS Bluefish July 1945. Showed Japan's supply crisis - building submarines without weapons to sneak cargo past Allied blockade.

**Key Specs:** 3,512/4,290 tons | 361ft | 15.8/6.3 kn | NO torpedoes | 300 tons cargo + 50-60 troops | 13,000 nm range | 3 boats

**Innovation:** Purpose-built transport submarines (not converted). Large cargo hold (300 tons capacity). Could carry troops (50-60), ammunition, food, fuel. No torpedo tubes (space used for cargo). Single shaft (simplicity). Minimal armament (one 25mm AA gun - defensive only). Designed to evade Allied air/surface blockade by traveling submerged. Late-war desperation - Japan's isolated garrisons starving, surface ships couldn't reach them, submarines only option. All three lost 1945 before accomplishing much. I-351 to USS Bluefish (July 1945). Others scuttled or lost. 100% loss rate. Showed strategic bankruptcy - submarines without weapons, carrying rice instead of torpedoes.

**Design:** Large submarine hull (361 ft, 3,512 tons surfaced). Huge cargo hold amidships (300 tons capacity). Could carry: ammunition, food, medical supplies, fuel, 50-60 troops. No torpedo tubes (cargo space priority). Single 25mm AA gun (minimal defense). Single shaft diesel-electric propulsion. Slow (15.8 knots surfaced, 6.3 submerged - penalty for cargo). Long range (13,000 nm - reach distant islands). Crew 90. Designed for supply runs to isolated garrisons: Philippines, Marianas, Carolines, Marshalls. Submerge when threatened, surface to unload cargo. Pure transport - combat avoidance priority.
