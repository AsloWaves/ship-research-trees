**Modern Landing Ship Dock Auxiliary** - Bay-class RFA vessels provide amphibious support 2006-present. 16,160 tons. Can carry 24 Challenger 2 tanks OR 150 trucks. Three active (one sold to Australia).

**Key Specs:** 16,160 tons | 579ft | 18 kn | 1 LCU + 2 LCVP | 24 MBTs | 356 troops (700 overload)

**Innovation:** Purpose-built for Royal Fleet Auxiliary to support amphibious operations. Can carry 24 Challenger 2 main battle tanks or 150 light trucks. 1,150 linear meters of vehicle space. Two 30-ton cranes for cargo operations. Supports Chinook and Merlin helicopter operations.

**Ships:**
- **RFA Mounts Bay (L3008)** - Commissioned 13 July 2006 (first). Vela Deployment 2006 (Equator to Arctic). Hurricane Irma relief 2017 (RFA Ship of Year). Littoral Response Group North 2024. Active.
- **RFA Largs Bay (L3006)** - Commissioned 28 Nov 2006. Falklands patrol 2008. Haiti earthquake relief 2010. **Sold to Australia 2011**, now HMAS Choules.
- **RFA Cardigan Bay (L3009)** - Commissioned 18 Dec 2006. Response Force Task Group. Yemen evacuation standby 2011. Minehunter mother ship operations. Active.
- **RFA Lyme Bay (L3007)** - Commissioned 26 Nov 2007 (last). 3-year Persian Gulf deployment from Bahrain. Tristan da Cunha emergency repairs 2008. Tropical Storm Erika relief 2015. Active.

**Service:** Primary Royal Fleet Auxiliary amphibious capability. Deployed globally for expeditionary operations, humanitarian relief, and amphibious support. Three-year Persian Gulf deployment (Lyme Bay). Caribbean hurricane relief operations. Falklands patrol duties.

**Capabilities:** Carries landing craft, vehicles, ammunition, and supplies. Flight deck operations for heavy helicopters. Can embark 356 troops normally, 700 in overload. Designed for sustained operations supporting Royal Marines and Army deployments.

**Australian Sale:** RFA Largs Bay sold to Royal Australian Navy in 2011 under Strategic Defence Review. Commissioned as HMAS Choules (L100) in December 2011, providing Australia with modern amphibious capability.
