**Cold War Logistics Ships** - Round Table-class (Sir Lancelot-class) were 6 RFA logistics landing ships 1963-2008. Roll-on/roll-off capability. 50 vehicles. Named after Knights of Round Table.

**Key Specs:** 5,674/6,700 tons | 449ft | 17 kn | 50 vehicles | 340 troops | Bow/stern ramps

**Innovation:** Featured roll-on/roll-off capability with bow and stern doors/ramps for rapid vehicle loading/unloading. Shallow draft allowed beaching operations. Could carry 16 light tanks plus 34 mixed vehicles, or 50 vehicles total. Also carried 122 tonnes fuel and 31 tonnes ammunition. Named after Arthurian Knights: Lancelot, Galahad, Bedivere, Geraint, Tristram, Percivale.

**Ships:**
- **RFA Sir Lancelot (L3029)** - Launched June 1963 (first)
- **RFA Sir Galahad (L3005)** - Launched April 1966, **lost at Fitzroy 8 June 1982 (Falklands War)**
- **RFA Sir Bedivere (L3004)** - Launched July 1966, last to decommission 2008
- **RFA Sir Geraint (L3006)** - Launched January 1967
- **RFA Sir Tristram (L3505)** - Launched December 1966, severely damaged Fitzroy 1982, rebuilt
- **RFA Sir Percivale (L3008)** - Launched October 1967

**Service:** Initially operated by British India Steam Navigation Company for British Army, transferred to Royal Fleet Auxiliary 1970. Provided logistics support for amphibious operations worldwide. Most famous service: 1982 Falklands War where Sir Galahad was lost and Sir Tristram severely damaged in Argentine air attack at Fitzroy (8 June 1982), with 48 British personnel killed. Sir Bedivere served until 2008 - longest service of class.

**Falklands Tragedy:** Sir Galahad anchored at Fitzroy with Welsh Guards aboard when attacked by Argentine A-4 Skyhawks. Hit by three bombs, ship caught fire. 48 killed (32 Welsh Guards, 11 ship's company, 5 others). Sir Tristram also hit but survived, later rebuilt and returned to service.
