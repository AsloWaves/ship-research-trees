# E-Class Submarines (1912-1917)

## Overview
The E-class were the most numerous British submarine class of WWI, with 58 boats built 1912-1917. They were successful fleet submarines based on the D-class diesel design, seeing extensive combat service in all theaters. The class achieved notable successes including operations in the Baltic, Dardanelles, and against German shipping, though with heavy losses.

## Design

**Fleet Submarine Capability:**
- Based on proven D-class
- Five torpedo tubes (2 bow, 2 beam, 1 stern)
- First with deck gun
- Enhanced range (3,000 nm)
- Better diving depth (300 ft)

**Key Features:**
- Beam torpedo tubes innovative
- Deck gun for surface combat
- Large torpedo loadout (10)
- Good seakeeping
- Reliable diesel engines

## WWI Service

**Major Theaters:**
- North Sea operations (extensive)
- Baltic Sea (E1, E8, E9, E13, E18, E19)
- Dardanelles/Sea of Marmara (E2, E7, E11, E14, E15, E20)
- Mediterranean operations
- Anti-U-boat patrols

**Baltic Operations:**
- 6 boats deployed 1914-1916
- E1 - Torpedoed German cruiser Moltke, August 1914
- E9 - Sank German cruiser Hela, September 1914
- Extreme conditions
- Strategic impact

**Dardanelles/Marmara:**
- E11 - Legendary operations, torpedoed battleship, gunboat, 11 steamers
- E14 - Extensive Marmara operations
- E15 - Lost attacking Turkish transport, April 1915
- Penetrated minefields
- Devastating to Turkish shipping

## Notable Achievements

**E11 - Most Successful:**
- Commander Martin Nasmith (VC)
- Multiple Marmara patrols
- Torpedoed battleship, destroyed 11 steamers
- Legendary status
- Most successful British submarine WWI

**E14 - Marmara Hero:**
- Commander Edward Boyle (VC)
- Multiple patrols
- Heavy Turkish losses
- Survived to armistice

**E9 - Baltic Success:**
- Sank German cruiser Hela
- Damaged cruiser Moltke (with E1)
- Effective operations

## Losses

**Heavy Casualties: 22 of 58 lost (38%):**

**Combat Losses:**
- E3 - Lost on patrol, 1916
- E6 - Mined North Sea, 1915
- E7 - Captured in Sea of Marmara, 1915
- E13 - Grounded, attacked by Germans (neutral waters violation), 1915
- E15 - Grounded Dardanelles, 1915
- E16 - Mined North Sea, 1916
- E17, E19, E20 - Various combat losses
- E24, E30, E34, E36, E37, E47, E49, E50 - Lost on patrol

**Baltic Scuttled:**
- E1, E8, E9, E18, E19 - Scuttled April 1918 (Russian Revolution)

## Post-WWI Fate

**Survivors:**
- Most scrapped 1921-1922
- Obsolete by armistice
- E11, E14 preserved briefly
- None survive today
