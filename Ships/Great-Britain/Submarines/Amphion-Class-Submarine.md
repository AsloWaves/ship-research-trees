# Amphion-Class (A-Class) Submarines (1945-1948)

## Overview
The Amphion-class (also known as A-class) were post-war fleet submarines with 16 boats built 1945-1948. They were improved wartime T-class designs with streamlined hulls, snorkels, and enhanced underwater performance. The class served extensively during the early Cold War and saw combat in the Suez Crisis and Malayan Emergency.

## Design

**Post-War Fleet Submarine:**
- Streamlined hull design
- Snorkel equipment fitted
- Improved underwater performance
- Based on wartime T-class
- Early Cold War capabilities

**Key Features:**
- Ten 21-inch torpedo tubes
- Exceptional range (10,500 nm)
- Snorkel for extended submerged operations
- Good surface speed (18.5 knots)
- Streamlined for better underwater performance

**Design Philosophy:**
- Incorporate wartime lessons
- Snorkel technology
- Improved habitability
- Fleet operations
- Cold War patrol duties

**Malayan Emergency:**

## Losses

**Single Loss:**
- **Affray** (A16) - Lost with all hands, April 1951 (75 killed)
  - Cause: Snorkel induction failure during diving
  - Largest loss of life in British submarine since WWII
  - Found in English Channel, 1951

**Low Loss Rate:**
- Only 1 of 16 lost (6%)
- Peacetime accident
- Snorkel design lessons
- Safety improvements implemented

## Modernizations

**Streamlining Programs (1950s-1960s):**
- Hull streamlining
- Conning tower modifications
- Removal of deck guns
- Improved battery capacity
- Snorkel improvements

## Preservation

**HMS Alliance:**
- Preserved at Royal Navy Submarine Museum, Gosport
- Only surviving A-class
- Museum ship and memorial
- Cold War submarine exhibit

## Post-Service Fate

**Retirement:**
- Most scrapped 1960s-1970s
- Replaced by Oberon-class
- Some served into 1970s
- 20-30 year service lives
