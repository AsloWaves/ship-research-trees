# Caroline-Class Light Cruisers (1915)

## Overview
The Caroline-class were the first group of the famous C-class light cruisers, representing a major improvement over the Arethusa-class. Six ships featured better armament and protection while maintaining high speed. They served extensively in WWI and several continued into WWII service.

## Design

**Improved Light Cruiser:**
- Larger than Arethusa-class
- Better armament layout
- Improved seakeeping
- First C-class group

**Key Features:**
- Ten main guns (2× 6-inch, 8× 4-inch)
- Better protection
- Enhanced range (4,000 nm)
- Oil-fired boilers

**Design Philosophy:**
- Flotilla leadership
- Fleet screening
- Trade protection
- Scout duties

## Ships
