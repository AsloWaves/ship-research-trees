# Devonshire-Class Armored Cruisers (1905)

## Overview
The Devonshire-class were improved County-type armored cruisers featuring enhanced protection and armament. Six ships combined the Monmouth-class's uniform 6-inch battery with better armor and speed. They served extensively through WWI in various roles including trade protection and fleet operations.

## Design

**Improved County-Type:**
- Mixed armament (7.5-inch and 6-inch)
- Enhanced armor (6-inch belt vs 4-inch)
- Better protection than Monmouth-class
- Maintained good range

**Key Features:**
- Compromise between heavy and uniform guns
- Improved protection
- Extended endurance
- Trade protection capable

## Notable Service

**HMS Hampshire Loss (June 5, 1916):**
- Carrying Lord Kitchener to Russia
- Hit mine off Orkney Islands
- Sank in 15 minutes
- 737 killed including Kitchener
- Only 12 survivors
- Major British loss

**WWI Operations:**
- Trade protection
- Northern Patrol
- Battle of Jutland area (some ships)
- Atlantic operations

## Legacy

**Design Assessment:**
- Better than Monmouth-class
- Improved armor protection
- Mixed armament more effective
- Good colonial cruiser

**Combat Record:**
- One major loss (Hampshire with Kitchener)
- Effective trade protection
- Long service lives

**Historical Significance:**
- Hampshire loss major event
- Lord Kitchener's death shocked Britain
- Conspiracy theories persisted for decades
