# Leander-Class Light Cruisers (1933)

## Overview
The Leander-class were modern light cruisers designed under London Naval Treaty limits. Eight ships (five RN, three transferred to Commonwealth navies) featured balanced design with good speed, range, and firepower. They served extensively through WWII and beyond.

## Design

**Modern Treaty Cruiser:**
- London Naval Treaty compliant
- Eight 6-inch guns in twin turrets
- Good balance of qualities
- Modern fire control

**Key Features:**
- All-forward main battery
- Enhanced AA armament
- Aircraft catapult
- Good range (10,000 nm)

**Design Philosophy:**
- Balanced light cruiser
- Fleet and trade protection
- Commonwealth service
- Modern capabilities

## Ships

### Royal Navy:
1. **HMS Leander** - Transferred to New Zealand 1937 (HMNZS Leander)
2. **HMS Achilles** - Transferred to New Zealand 1936 (HMNZS Achilles)
3. **HMS Ajax** - Scrapped 1949
4. **HMS Neptune** - **SUNK** 1941 (mine, Mediterranean)
5. **HMS Orion** - Scrapped 1949

### Commonwealth:
6. **HMAS Perth** (ex-Amphion) - **SUNK** 1942 (Battle of Sunda Strait)
7. **HMAS Hobart** - Scrapped 1962
8. **HMAS Sydney** - **SUNK** 1941 (HSK Kormoran)

## Notable Service

**Battle of River Plate (December 13, 1939):**
- HMNZS Achilles and HMS Ajax engaged Graf Spee
- With HMS Exeter forced Graf Spee to scuttle
- Major early war victory
- Both ships damaged but effective

**HMAS Sydney Loss (November 19, 1941):**
- Engaged German raider HSK Kormoran
- Both ships sunk
- All 645 crew lost
- Australia's greatest naval disaster
