# Orlando-Class Protected Cruisers (1888)

## Overview
The Orlando-class were first-class protected cruisers built for commerce protection and fleet duties. Seven ships combined good speed with heavy armament, representing a significant advance in British cruiser design. They served extensively until WWI, with some seeing wartime service.

## Design

**First-Class Protected Cruiser:**
- Heavy main armament (9.2-inch)
- Strong secondary battery
- Triple expansion engines
- Enhanced deck armor

**Key Features:**
- Powerful broadside
- Good endurance
- Suitable for fleet operations
- Commerce protection capable

## Service

**Pre-WWI:**
- Fleet duties
- Training ship roles
- Colonial service
- Trade route protection

**Legacy:**
- Successful first-class cruiser design
- Long service (17-25 years)
- Influenced subsequent protected cruiser designs
