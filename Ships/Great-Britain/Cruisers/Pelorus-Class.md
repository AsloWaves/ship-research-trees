# Pelorus-Class Protected Cruisers (1897)

## Overview
The Pelorus-class were third-class protected cruisers designed for colonial patrol and trade protection. Eleven ships were built, representing the Royal Navy's standard small cruiser for overseas service. They saw extensive service through WWI and beyond, with several serving over 30 years.

## Design

**Third-Class Protected Cruiser:**
- Small size for colonial patrol
- Good speed (20 knots)
- Cost-effective design
- Eleven ships built

**Key Features:**
- Eight 4-inch guns
- Light protection
- Extended range
- Suitable for independent operations

**Pre-WWI:**
- Colonial station service
- Trade protection
- Anti-piracy patrols
- Diplomatic missions

- HMS Pegasus sunk by SMS Königsberg (Sep 1914)
- East Africa operations
- Trade protection
- Training duties

**Legacy:**
- Successful colonial cruiser design
- One WWI loss in action
- Long service lives (up to 26 years)
- Final third-class protected cruiser type
