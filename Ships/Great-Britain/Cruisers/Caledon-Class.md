# Caledon-Class Light Cruisers (1917)

## Overview
The Caledon-class were a four-ship subgroup of the C-class light cruisers featuring improved armament with all 6-inch guns. They represented the final evolution of the C-class design with lessons learned from wartime experience.

## Design

**Improved C-Class:**
- All 6-inch main battery
- Better range (5,900 nm)
- Slightly larger
- Wartime lessons incorporated

**Key Features:**
- Uniform 6-inch armament
- Enhanced anti-aircraft capability
- Improved fire control
- Better seakeeping

**Design Evolution:**
- Final C-class variant
- Mixed battery eliminated
- AA defense added
- Greater endurance

## Ships

1. **HMS Caledon** - Scrapped 1948
2. **HMS Calypso** - **SUNK** 1940 (torpedo, Mediterranean)
3. **HMS Caradoc** - Scrapped 1946
4. **HMS Cassandra** - **SUNK** 1918 (mine, Baltic)

## Service

**WWI:**
- Late WWI entry
- Limited WWI action
- HMS Cassandra mined 1918

**Interwar:**
- Foreign station service
- Modernization in 1930s
- Continued active service

**WWII:**
- Three survived to WWII
- HMS Calypso torpedoed 1940
- Caledon and Caradoc survived war
- Effective convoy escorts

## Legacy

**Design Assessment:**
- Best C-class variant
- All 6-inch armament superior
- Good interwar service
- Served in two world wars

**Historical Significance:**
- Final C-class development
- Longest service lives of C-class
- Combat veterans of both wars
- Showed value of uniform battery
