# Minotaur-Class Armored Cruisers (1908)

## Overview
The Minotaur-class were the largest and last British armored cruisers, featuring heavy armament and improved protection. Three ships represented the ultimate development of the armored cruiser concept before battlecruisers made them obsolete. They served through WWI in various roles including fleet operations and trade protection.

## Design

**Ultimate Armored Cruiser:**
- Largest British armored cruisers
- Heavy mixed armament
- Enhanced protection
- Final development before battlecruisers

**Key Features:**
- 4× 9.2-inch main guns
- 10× 7.5-inch secondary battery
- Powerful broadside
- Last of their type

**Design Philosophy:**
- Counter foreign armored cruisers
- Fleet screening role
- Trade protection capable
- Soon made obsolete by battlecruisers

## Ships

1. **HMS Minotaur** - Scrapped 1920
2. **HMS Defence** (different from Warrior-class Defence) - Actually Warrior-class
3. **HMS Shannon** - Scrapped 1922

**Note:** Only Minotaur and Shannon were Minotaur-class. Defence was Warrior-class (a source of confusion).

## WWI Service

**Operations:**
- Grand Fleet service
- Trade protection
- Northern Patrol
- Fleet screening
- Atlantic operations

**Notable Actions:**
- No major fleet engagements
- Effective trade protection
- Survived the war

## Legacy

**Design Assessment:**
- Powerful armored cruiser design
- Too expensive for role
- Made obsolete by battlecruisers
- Good trade protection ships

**Historical Significance:**
- Last British armored cruisers built
- Largest armored cruisers ever built for RN
- End of armored cruiser era
- Battlecruisers replaced the type

**Evolution:**
- Armored cruiser concept peaked
- Battlecruisers offered better speed and protection
- WWI showed armored cruisers too vulnerable for fleet action
- Better suited to trade protection than fleet battle
