# Tiger-Class Helicopter Cruisers (1959-1978)

## Overview
HMS Tiger and HMS Blake were Minotaur-class cruisers completed with major modifications as helicopter cruisers. HMS Tiger was further converted in the 1970s to embark Sea King helicopters and serve as a commando carrier. They represented the Royal Navy's last gun cruisers and transition to helicopter operations.

## Design

**Original Completion (1959-1961):**
- Completed from wartime hulls
- Modified Minotaur design
- Reduced main armament
- Modern electronics

**1970s Tiger Conversion:**
- HMS Tiger converted 1968-1972
- Hangar and flight deck added
- Four Sea King helicopters
- Commando carrier role
- Forward guns retained

## Ships

1. **HMS Tiger (C20)**
   - Completed 1959
   - Converted to helicopter cruiser 1968-1972
   - Decommissioned 1978
   - Scrapped 1986

2. **HMS Blake (C99)**
   - Completed 1961
   - Converted to helicopter cruiser 1965-1969
   - Decommissioned 1979
   - Scrapped 1982

## Service History

## Conversion Details

**Tiger Conversion (1968-1972):**
- Hangar for 4× Sea King
- Flight deck aft
- Forward 6-inch turrets retained
- Commando accommodation
- Modern radar and communications

**Blake Conversion (1965-1969):**
- Similar to Tiger
- 4× Wessex helicopters initially
- Later Sea King capable
- Amphibious warfare role
