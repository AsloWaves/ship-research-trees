# Diadem-Class Protected Cruisers (1898)

## Overview
The Diadem-class were first-class protected cruisers built for trade protection and fleet operations. Eight ships served extensively in WWI, with several seeing action in major battles. They represented the mature protected cruiser design before the shift to armored cruisers.

## Design

**Mature Protected Cruiser:**
- Uniform 6-inch battery (16 guns)
- Large size (11,000 tons)
- Good speed and range
- Last generation before armored cruisers

**Key Features:**
- Heavy broadside with 16× 6-inch guns
- Armored deck protection
- Suitable for trade protection
- Fleet screening capability

## Ships

1. HMS Diadem - Scrapped 1921
2. HMS Niobe - Transferred to Canada 1910, scrapped 1920
3. HMS Amphitrite - Scrapped 1920
4. HMS Ariadne - Scrapped 1920

7. HMS Spartiate - Scrapped 1932

## Service

**Pre-WWI:**
- Colonial station service
- Trade protection
- Training duties
- HMS Niobe transferred to Royal Canadian Navy

**WWI:**
- North Sea patrol
- Trade protection
- Convoy escort
- Training and auxiliary roles

**Legacy:**
- Last large protected cruiser class
- Extensive WWI service
- Long service lives (up to 58 years for Andromeda)
- Demonstrated viability of 6-inch uniform battery
