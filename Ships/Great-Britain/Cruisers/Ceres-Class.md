# Ceres-Class Light Cruisers (1917)

## Overview
The Ceres-class were a five-ship subgroup of the C-class light cruisers very similar to the Caledon-class. They featured the improved all 6-inch armament and served through both world wars.

## Design

**Late C-Class:**
- Nearly identical to Caledon-class
- All 6-inch armament
- Good range and speed
- AA capability

**Key Features:**
- Uniform main battery
- Anti-aircraft guns
- Improved design
- Wartime construction

## Ships

1. **HMS Ceres** - Scrapped 1946
2. **HMS Cardiff** - Scrapped 1946
3. **HMS Coventry** - **SUNK** 1942 (dive bombers, Mediterranean)
4. **HMS Curacoa** - **SUNK** 1942 (collision with Queen Mary)
5. **HMS Curlew** - **SUNK** 1940 (air attack, Norway)

## Service

**WWI:**
- Late entry to service
- Limited WWI operations
- All survived WWI

**Interwar:**
- Foreign station service
- Some modernization
- Active fleet service

**WWII:**
- Three lost in WWII
- HMS Curacoa rammed by Queen Mary (1942)
- HMS Curlew sunk off Norway (1940)
- HMS Coventry bombed in Mediterranean (1942)
- Ceres and Cardiff survived war

## Legacy

**Design Assessment:**
- Successful late C-class
- Good wartime service
- Two served both wars
- Effective light cruisers

**Combat Record:**
- Heavy WWII losses (3 of 5)
- HMS Curacoa loss tragic (rammed by liner)
- Effective convoy escorts
- Valuable fleet units
