# Challenger-Class Protected Cruisers (1902)

## Overview
The Challenger-class were third-class protected cruisers designed for scouting and colonial patrol duties. Two ships represented the final evolution of the small protected cruiser, featuring improved speed and modern quick-firing guns. They served extensively in WWI in various roles.

## Design

**Final Protected Cruiser Type:**
- Quick-firing 6-inch guns
- Enhanced speed (21 knots)
- Scouting role
- Transitional design

**Key Features:**
- Modern quick-firing armament
- Good speed for scouting
- Extended range
- Last third-class protected cruisers

## Ships

1. **HMS Challenger** - Scrapped 1920
2. **HMS Encounter** - Transferred RAN 1912, scrapped 1932

## Service

**Pre-WWI:**
- Fleet scouting duties
- Colonial station service
- HMS Encounter transferred to Royal Australian Navy

**WWI:**
- Trade protection
- Convoy escort
- Training duties
- Australian waters (Encounter)

**Legacy:**
- Last protected cruiser design
- Transitional to scout cruiser concept
- Long service (16-30 years)
- Demonstrated shift toward light cruiser role
