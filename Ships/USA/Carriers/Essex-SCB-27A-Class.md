**Jet Conversion** - Essex SCB-27A modernization adapted 9 carriers for jet operations. Strengthened flight deck, improved catapults, new elevators. First major Essex modernization 1951-1955.

**Key Specs:** 30,800/40,600 tons | 888ft | 31 kn | 70-80 jets | 150,000 shp | H-8 hydraulic catapults

**Innovation:** First Essex modernization for jet operations. Strengthened flight deck for heavier jets. New H-8 hydraulic catapults. Relocated elevators. Improved arresting gear. Enhanced aviation fuel capacity. Stronger deck structure. Extended service life 20+ years.

**Modernization Changes:**
- Strengthened flight deck for 40,000 lb jets
- H-8 hydraulic catapults (capacity increased)
- Relocated port elevator to deck edge
- New radar and electronics
- Enhanced aviation fuel systems
- Improved crew spaces
- Rebuilt island structure

**Ships Modernized:**
CV-10 Yorktown, CV-11 Intrepid, CV-12 Hornet, CV-15 Randolph, CV-16 Lexington, CV-19 Hancock, CV-20 Bennington, CV-31 Bon Homme Richard, CV-33 Kearsarge

**Service:** Korean War. Vietnam War. Many served as ASW carriers (CVS) 1960s-1970s. Training carriers. Last decommissioned 1976 (Lexington served until 1991 after further upgrades).
