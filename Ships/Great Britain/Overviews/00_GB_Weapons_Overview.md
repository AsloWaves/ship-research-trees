## Technology Evolution

### WWI (1914-1920)
- **Primitive bombs:** Hales 20 lb, RL 112 lb, 65 lb
- **No guidance:** Simple gravity drop
- **First torpedo attack:** Short 184 with 14-inch torpedo (1915)
- **Accuracy:** Very poor, visual aiming only

### Interwar (1920-1938)
- **Improved bombs:** Medium Capacity series developed
- **Torpedo standardization:** 18-inch Mk XII aerial torpedo
- **Better sights:** Mechanical bomb sights
- **Semi-Armor Piercing:** SAP bombs for ship attack

### WWII (1939-1945)
- **RP-3 Rockets:** 3-inch unguided rockets (1942-1943)
- **Improved torpedoes:** Mk XV with higher performance
- **Depth charges:** Aerial ASW weapons (1941-1942)
- **Better explosives:** Torpex replacing TNT

### Early Guided Era (1957-1990)
- **First AAMs:** Firestreak (1957), Red Top (1964)
- **Radar missiles:** Skyflash (1978)
- **Anti-ship missiles:** Sea Eagle (1985)
- **Laser-guided bombs:** Paveway II/III (1976/1983)

### Modern Precision (1990-Present)
- **Fire-and-forget AAMs:** AMRAAM (1991), ASRAAM (1998), Meteor (2016)
- **Dual-mode guidance:** Paveway IV GPS/Laser (2008)
- **Precision strike:** Brimstone mmW/SAL (2005)
- **Sub-meter accuracy:** Modern PGMs with CEP <1m

## Weapon Compatibility System

### Technology Gap Rule
Weapons introduced >25 years after aircraft are **INCOMPATIBLE**
- Prevents anachronistic loadouts
- Maintains historical accuracy

### Fire Control Requirements

| FCS Gen | Weapons Compatible |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Gen 0** | Unguided bombs, torpedoes, depth charges, rockets (pre-electrical only) |
| **Gen 1** | Gen 0 + electrical rockets (RP-3, etc.) |
| **Gen 2** | Gen 1 + early AAMs (Firestreak, Red Top, AIM-9, Skyflash) |
| **Gen 3** | Gen 2 + modern PGMs, databus weapons (AMRAAM, ASRAAM, Meteor, Paveway IV, Brimstone) |

### Electrical Requirements
- **Pre-1940 aircraft:** No electrical interface = no electrically-fuzed weapons
- **Pre-1945 jets:** Cannot use guided missiles
- **Modern aircraft:** Require databus for AMRAAM, ASRAAM, Meteor, Paveway IV

### Physical Constraints
- **Weight limits:** Weapon must not exceed aircraft max ordnance weight
- **Hardpoint limits:** Weapon weight ÷ hardpoints must be reasonable
- **Example:** Swordfish (4 hardpoints, 2000 lbs max) can carry:
  - 1x 18-in Mk XII torpedo (1548 lbs) ✓
  - 4x 250 lb bombs (1000 lbs total) ✓
  - 8x RP-3 rockets (572 lbs total) ✓

## Notable Combat Uses

### WWI
- **First torpedo sinking:** Short 184 with torpedo (1915)
- **Zeppelin attacks:** Sopwith Pup/Camel with incendiary bombs

### WWII
- **Taranto (Nov 1940):** Swordfish with torpedoes & bombs cripple Italian fleet
- **Bismarck (May 1941):** Swordfish torpedo jams rudder, enables sinking
- **Tirpitz (Apr 1944):** Barracudas with bombs (Operation Tungsten)
- **RP-3 Rockets:** Seafires & Sea Hurricanes vs U-boats & ships

### Falklands War (1982)
- **AIM-9L Sidewinder:** 87% kill rate (21 of 24 launches), zero air-to-air losses
- **BL755 Cluster Bombs:** Sea Harriers vs airfields & ground targets
- **Sea Eagle:** Anti-ship capability (not used in anger in Falklands)

### Afghanistan/Iraq (2001-2014)
- **Paveway II/IV:** Precision strikes by Sea Harriers & Harrier GR7/9
- **Brimstone:** Tank/vehicle strikes with exceptional accuracy
