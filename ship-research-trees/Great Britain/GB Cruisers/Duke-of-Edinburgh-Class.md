# Duke of Edinburgh-Class Armored Cruisers (1906)

## Overview
The Duke of Edinburgh-class were advanced County-type armored cruisers featuring improved speed and armament. Two ships represented the final evolution of the County design with mixed 9.2-inch and 6-inch batteries. They served through WWI and into the 1920s.

## Design

**Advanced County-Type:**
- Heavy main armament (6× 9.2-inch)
- Good secondary battery (10× 6-inch)
- Enhanced protection
- Good speed (23 knots)

**Key Features:**
- Largest County-type design
- Most powerful County armament
- Better armor than earlier Counties
- Improved machinery

## Ships

1. **HMS Duke of Edinburgh** - Scrapped 1920
2. **HMS Black Prince** - **SUNK** 1916 (Battle of Jutland)

## Notable Service

**HMS Black Prince at Jutland (May 31, 1916):**
- Night phase of battle
- Wandered into German battle line in darkness
- Destroyed by point-blank fire
- Exploded and sank
- All 857 hands lost
- No survivors

**WWI Operations:**
- Grand Fleet service
- Trade protection
- Northern Patrol
- Fleet screening

## Legacy

**Design Assessment:**
- Powerful armored cruiser
- Good balance of speed and firepower
- Better than earlier County-types
- Still outclassed by battlecruisers

**Combat Record:**
- Black Prince loss at Jutland tragic
- Demonstrated danger to old cruisers in night action
- Duke of Edinburgh survived war

**Historical Significance:**
- Last County-type development
- Black Prince one of three British armored cruisers lost at Jutland
- Showed vulnerability of armored cruisers in fleet action
