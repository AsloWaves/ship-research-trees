# Blake-Class Protected Cruisers (1892)

## Overview
The Blake-class were large first-class protected cruisers designed for commerce protection and distant station service. HMS Blake and HMS Blenheim were among the largest protected cruisers built, featuring powerful armament and exceptional range. They served into WWI in secondary roles.

## Design

**Large Protected Cruiser:**
- Among largest protected cruisers built
- Heavy armament for commerce protection
- Exceptional range
- Enhanced speed (22 knots)

**Key Features:**
- Powerful broadside with 9.2-inch guns
- Thick armored deck (up to 6 inches)
- Turret armor protection
- Designed to hunt down commerce raiders

## Ships

1. **HMS Blake** - Scrapped 1922
2. **HMS Blenheim** - Scrapped 1926

## Service

**Pre-WWI:**
- Trade protection duties
- Colonial station service
- Fleet operations

**WWI Service:**
- Training ship roles
- Depot ship duties
- Long service lives (30+ years)

**Legacy:**
- Demonstrated limits of protected cruiser concept
- Too large and expensive for peacetime
- Last of the large protected cruisers
