# Amphion-Class Light Cruisers (1936)

## Overview
The Amphion-class (also called Perth-class or modified Leander-class) were improved Leander-class cruisers with better protection and increased displacement. Four ships built for the Royal Australian Navy featured enhanced capabilities while maintaining the successful Leander design.

## Design

**Modified Leander:**
- Improved protection (4-inch belt vs 3.5-inch)
- Better deck armor (2 inches)
- Slightly larger displacement
- Enhanced survivability

**Key Features:**
- Enhanced armor scheme
- Same armament as Leander
- Good speed maintained
- Australian service

**Design Philosophy:**
- Lessons from Leander experience
- Improved protection priority
- Maintain capabilities
- RAN requirements

## Ships

1. **HMS Amphion** - Transferred to RAN 1939 (HMAS Perth), **SUNK** 1942
2. **HMAS Sydney** - **SUNK** 1941 (HSK Kormoran)
3. **HMAS Hobart** - Scrapped 1962
4. **HMAS Perth** (original) - Renamed HMAS Amphion, see above

## Notable Service

**HMAS Sydney Loss (November 19, 1941):**
- Engaged German raider HSK Kormoran
- Both ships sunk in mutual destruction
- All 645 Australian crew lost
- Australia's greatest naval loss

**HMAS Perth Loss (March 1, 1942):**
- Battle of Sunda Strait
- With USS Houston attacked Japanese invasion fleet
- Both Allied ships sunk
- 353 of 681 crew lost
- Survivors POWs

**HMAS Hobart:**
- Extensive Pacific service
- Survived entire war
- Served into 1960s
- Only class survivor
