# Acasta-Class Destroyers (K-Class) (1912)

## Overview
The Acasta-class (K-Class) were the ultimate pre-WWI British destroyers, with 20 ships built 1912-1913. They featured significantly increased size and power, introducing the 1,000-ton destroyer standard and three 4-inch guns. The class represented the culmination of pre-war destroyer development and served as the immediate predecessors to WWI emergency classes.

## Design

**Advanced Features:**
- Approached 1,000 tons
- Three 4-inch guns (vs two previously)
- Higher power (24,500 vs 13,500 shp)
- Twin torpedo tube mounts

**Key Characteristics:**
- Largest pre-WWI destroyers
- Improved firepower
- Higher speed (29 knots)
- Enhanced endurance

**Design Philosophy:**
- Fleet destroyer standard
- Superior firepower emphasis
- High speed capability
- Pre-war peak design
