**Germany's First Standardized Light Cruiser Class** - 6 ships built (1890-1893): Bussard, Falke, Seeadler, Cormoran, Condor, Geier. Last unprotected cruisers built for Kaiserliche Marine. 1,612 tons, 6× 10.5cm guns, 15.5 knots. Designed for colonial service in China, East Africa, and Pacific stations. Established successful pattern for German overseas cruisers. Notable service: Geier was only German cruiser interned in USA (Honolulu, April 1917, seized when USA entered WWI). All served in WWI colonial stations, most scuttled or captured 1914-1917.

**Key Features:** First standardized German cruiser class (6 ships) | Colonial service specialists | Geier: Only German cruiser interned in USA | Last unprotected cruisers

**Ships:** SMS Bussard, SMS Falke, SMS Seeadler, SMS Cormoran (scuttled Guam 1917), SMS Condor, SMS Geier (interned Honolulu 1917, seized by USA)

**Service:** Colonial stations (1890-1914): China (Bussard, Falke, Cormoran), East Africa (Seeadler, Condor), Pacific (Geier). WWI service: Most scuttled or captured 1914-1917. Geier: Interned Honolulu April 1917, seized when USA entered WWI, became USS Schurz (sunk by collision 1918). Cormoran: Scuttled Guam April 1917.
