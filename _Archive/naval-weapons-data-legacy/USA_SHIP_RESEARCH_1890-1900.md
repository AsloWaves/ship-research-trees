# USA Naval Ship Research Tree: 1890-1900
## The Birth of the Modern US Navy - "New Steel Navy" Era

**Historical Context**: This decade marks the transformation of the US Navy from wooden sailing ships to modern steel warships. The Naval Act of 1890 and the Spanish-American War (1898) drove rapid expansion.

---

## Ship Categories Available (1890-1900)

### ✅ Available
- **Battleships** - First true steel battleships
- **Cruisers** - Protected and armored cruisers
- **Torpedo Boats** - Precursors to destroyers
- **Submarines** - Experimental, one commissioned (1900)
- **Auxiliaries** - Cargo, colliers, tenders

### ❌ Not Yet Developed
- **Destroyers** - First true destroyers appear 1902 (Bainbridge-class)
- **Aircraft Carriers** - Won't exist until WWI/1920s

## Research Tree Structure (1890-1900)

### Tier 1: Pre-Dreadnought Battleships (1890-1895)

#### **Indiana-class Battleship** (BB-1 through BB-3) - 1893-1895
**Research Unlock**: Starting tech (available immediately)
**Ships**:
- USS Indiana (BB-1) - Commissioned 1895
- USS Massachusetts (BB-2) - Commissioned 1896
- USS Oregon (BB-3) - Commissioned 1896

**Specifications**:
- **Displacement**: 10,288 tons (standard)
- **Length**: 348 ft
- **Beam**: 69.3 ft
- **Draft**: 24 ft
- **Speed**: 15 knots (designed), 16.79 knots (trial)
- **Range**: 4,900 nm at 10 knots
- **Crew**: 32 officers, 441 enlisted = 473 total
- **Armor**:
  - Belt: 18" amidships, 8" ends
  - Deck: 2.75" flat, 3" slopes
  - Turrets: 15" face, 8.5" sides, 3" roof
  - Conning tower: 7.5"
- **Main Armament**: 4× 13"/35 caliber guns (2× twin turrets)
- **Secondary**: 8× 8"/35, 4× 6"/40 guns
- **Torpedo Tubes**: 2× 18" submerged

**Historical Notes**:
- First true American battleships
- Low freeboard led to poor seakeeping
- Oregon's famous 14,000-mile voyage around Cape Horn (1898)
- All served in Spanish-American War
- Indiana and Massachusetts became training ships post-war

**Research Cost**: 5,000 (starting tech)
**Build Time**: 36 months
**Build Cost**: $3,020,000 (1890s USD)

---

#### **Iowa-class Battleship (1896)** (BB-4) - NOT WWII Iowa!
**Research Unlock**: Requires Indiana-class battleship
**Prerequisites**: Indiana-class
**Ships**:
- USS Iowa (BB-4) - Commissioned 1897

**Specifications**:
- **Displacement**: 11,410 tons (standard), 12,647 tons (full load)
- **Length**: 362.5 ft overall
- **Beam**: 72.25 ft
- **Draft**: 24 ft
- **Speed**: 16 knots (designed), 17.09 knots (trial)
- **Range**: 5,640 nm at 10 knots
- **Crew**: 31 officers, 515 enlisted = 546 total
- **Armor**:
  - Belt: 14" amidships, 4" ends
  - Deck: 2.75" flat, 3" slopes
  - Turrets: 15" face, 12.5" sides, 3" roof
  - Barbettes: 15"
  - Conning tower: 10"
- **Main Armament**: 4× 12"/40 caliber guns (2× twin turrets)
- **Secondary**: 8× 8"/35, 6× 4"/40 guns
- **Torpedo Tubes**: 4× 18" above water

**Improvements over Indiana-class**:
- Larger size, better freeboard, improved seakeeping
- Different caliber main guns (12" vs 13")
- Higher speed
- Better armor distribution

**Historical Notes**:
- Sole ship of her class
- Spanish-American War service
- Training ship 1903-1919
- Converted to radio-controlled target ship 1919
- Sunk as target 1923

**Research Cost**: 8,000
**Build Time**: 30 months
**Build Cost**: $3,010,000
**Unlocks**: Kearsarge-class

---

#### **Kearsarge-class Battleship** (BB-5, BB-6) - 1898-1900
**Research Unlock**: Requires Iowa-class (BB-4)
**Prerequisites**: Iowa-class (BB-4)
**Ships**:
- USS Kearsarge (BB-5) - Commissioned 1900
- USS Kentucky (BB-6) - Commissioned 1900

**Specifications**:
- **Displacement**: 11,540 tons (standard), 13,500 tons (full load)
- **Length**: 375.4 ft overall
- **Beam**: 72.2 ft
- **Draft**: 23.5 ft
- **Speed**: 16 knots (designed), 16.84 knots (trial)
- **Range**: 5,850 nm at 10 knots
- **Crew**: 40 officers, 521 enlisted = 561 total
- **Armor**:
  - Belt: 16.5" amidships, 5" ends
  - Deck: 2.75" flat, 3" slopes
  - Main turrets: 17" face, 15" sides, 3" roof
  - Secondary turrets: 11" face, 8" sides
  - Barbettes: 15"
  - Conning tower: 10"
- **Main Armament**: 4× 13"/35 caliber guns (2× twin turrets)
- **Secondary**: 4× 8"/35 guns (mounted ABOVE main turrets - unique!)
- **Additional**: 14× 5"/40, 20× 6-pdr, 8× 1-pdr guns
- **Torpedo Tubes**: 4× 18" above water

**Unique Feature**: **Double-turret design** - 8" guns mounted on top of 13" turrets
- Innovative but problematic
- Blast interference between turrets
- Never repeated in US Navy

**Historical Notes**:
- Experimental superimposed turret arrangement
- Concept abandoned due to blast issues
- Great White Fleet world cruise (1907-1909)
- Training ship post-1915
- Scrapped 1923

**Research Cost**: 10,000
**Build Time**: 36 months
**Build Cost**: $3,760,000
**Unlocks**: Illinois-class (1898-1901, outside this timeframe)

#### **Olympia-class Protected Cruiser** (C-6) - 1895
**Research Unlock**: Requires Baltimore-class
**Prerequisites**: Baltimore-class
**Ships**:
- USS Olympia (C-6) - Commissioned 1895

**Specifications**:
- **Displacement**: 5,865 tons (standard), 6,558 tons (full load)
- **Length**: 344 ft overall
- **Beam**: 53 ft
- **Draft**: 21.5 ft
- **Speed**: 21.68 knots (trial) - Fast for era!
- **Range**: 11,678 nm at 8.5 knots
- **Crew**: 33 officers, 396 enlisted = 429 total
- **Armor**:
  - Protected deck: 4.75" slopes, 2" flat
  - Conning tower: 5"
  - Gun shields: 4.5"
- **Main Armament**: 4× 8"/35 caliber guns (single mounts)
- **Secondary**: 10× 5"/40 guns
- **Additional**: 14× 6-pdr, 6× 1-pdr, 4× Gatling guns
- **Torpedo Tubes**: 6× 18"

**Fame**: ⭐ **Admiral Dewey's flagship at Battle of Manila Bay (May 1, 1898)**
- "You may fire when you are ready, Gridley"
- Destroyed Spanish Pacific fleet without losing a single American life

**Historical Notes**:
- One of the finest protected cruisers ever built
- Excellent speed and endurance
- Now preserved as museum ship in Philadelphia (oldest steel US warship afloat)
- National Historic Landmark

**Research Cost**: 5,000
**Build Time**: 24 months
**Build Cost**: $1,796,000
**Unlocks**: Columbia-class

**Research Cost**: 7,000

### Tier 5: Submarines (1898-1900)

#### **Holland VI (SS-1)** - 1900
**Research Unlock**: Requires advanced research investment
**Prerequisites**: None (revolutionary new technology)
**Ships**:
- USS Holland (SS-1) - Commissioned 1900 (October 12)

**Specifications**:
- **Displacement**: 63 tons surfaced, 74 tons submerged
- **Length**: 53.3 ft
- **Beam**: 10.3 ft
- **Draft**: 8.5 ft
- **Speed**: 8 knots surfaced, 5 knots submerged
- **Range**: 1,000 nm at 7 knots surfaced
- **Endurance**: 4 hours submerged
- **Crew**: 1 officer, 5 enlisted = 6 total
- **Propulsion**:
  - Surfaced: 45 hp gasoline engine (Otto type)
  - Submerged: 50 hp electric motor
- **Diving Depth**: 75 ft
- **Armament**:
  - 1× 18" torpedo tube (bow)
  - 3× Whitehead torpedoes (carried)
  - 1× 8" pneumatic dynamite gun (deck gun)

**Revolutionary Features**:
- First submarine commissioned by US Navy
- Combination gasoline/electric propulsion
- Hydroplanes for depth control
- Practical submerged endurance

**Historical Context**:
- Designed by John Philip Holland (Irish immigrant)
- US Navy initially skeptical
- Proved viability of submarine warfare
- Foundation of US submarine force

**Historical Notes**:
- Demonstrated to President McKinley and naval officials
- Successful attack simulations on battleships
- Retired 1905 after proving concept
- Now on display at Paterson Museum, New Jersey

**Research Cost**: 20,000 (expensive experimental tech)
**Build Time**: 24 months (prototype development)
**Build Cost**: $150,000 (prototype cost)
**Unlocks**: A-class submarines (1901-1903)

## Historical Timeline (1890-1900)

**1890**
- Naval Act of 1890 authorizes first seagoing battleships
- USS Baltimore commissioned

**1893**
- Indiana-class battleships laid down (BB-1, BB-2, BB-3)

**1895**
- USS Indiana (BB-1) commissioned - First true US battleship
- USS Olympia commissioned

**1896**
- USS Massachusetts (BB-2) commissioned
- USS Oregon (BB-3) commissioned
- USS Brooklyn (ACR-3) commissioned

**1897**
- USS Iowa (BB-4) commissioned

**1898**
- **Spanish-American War** (April-August)
  - Battle of Manila Bay (May 1) - Olympia leads victory
  - Battle of Santiago (July 3) - Brooklyn's famous "Brooklyn Loop"
- Kearsarge-class laid down

**1900**
- USS Kearsarge (BB-5) commissioned
- USS Kentucky (BB-6) commissioned
- **USS Holland (SS-1) commissioned** - First US submarine (October 12)

## Game Integration Suggestions

### Research Prerequisites
1. **Linear Progression** within ship types (BB → better BB)
2. **Cross-Tech Requirements**:
   - Armored cruisers require protected cruisers
   - Submarines are standalone revolutionary tech
   - Destroyers (1902+) require torpedo boat experience

### Balance Mechanics
- **Starting techs**: Indiana BB, Baltimore cruiser, Cushing torpedo boat
- **Mid-tier**: Iowa BB, Olympia cruiser, Porter torpedo boat
- **Advanced**: Kearsarge BB, Brooklyn armored cruiser
- **Revolutionary**: Holland submarine (expensive, game-changing)

### Resource System
- **Research Points**: Earned through time/battles
- **Build Resources**: Steel, coal, labor
- **Maintenance Costs**: Crew salaries, fuel, ammunition

### Historical Bonuses
- **Olympia**: +10% accuracy at Manila Bay scenario
- **Oregon**: +15% endurance (long voyage bonus)
- **Brooklyn**: Special maneuver ability (Brooklyn Loop)
- **Holland**: Stealth attack bonus
