First ocean-going destroyers since WWI. **Named after naval heroes** (Leberecht Maass = German admiral). **Stability problems** (top-weight issues plagued class). WWII service (1939-1944): Norway campaign, Atlantic operations, English Channel. **Heavy combat losses** (Z1/Z3 lost 1940, others 1944). Nazi rearmament milestone.

WWII service (1939-1944): **Norway campaign** (1940 - Z1 and Z3 lost in First Battle of Narvik, 10 April 1940), Atlantic convoy operations, **English Channel operations** (1940-1944), North Sea patrols, mine-laying missions. Stability problems from excessive top-weight (high bridge, heavy armament) caused handling difficulties in heavy seas. Despite flaws, **Type 1934** represented revolutionary capability jump: first ocean-going destroyers since WWI V105-class (1916), triple the displacement of treaty boats, modern 127mm guns, powerful torpedo armament. **Heavy combat losses**: Z1 and Z3 lost 1940 Narvik (British destroyers), Z2 and Z4 survived until 1944 (air attack, mining). Type 1934 established template for all subsequent German WWII destroyers: large size, heavy armament, high speed, but stability issues led to Type 1934A improvements.
