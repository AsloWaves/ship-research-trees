# Drake-Class Armored Cruisers (1902)

## Overview
The Drake-class were improved armored cruisers featuring enhanced armament and speed. Four ships combined heavy firepower with good seakeeping, serving extensively through WWI. They represented a significant improvement over the Cressy-class with better speed and more powerful armament.

## Design

**Improved Armored Cruiser:**
- Enhanced speed (23 knots)
- Increased secondary battery (16× 6-inch)
- Larger hull for better seakeeping
- More powerful machinery

**Key Features:**
- Heavy broadside with 16× 6-inch guns
- Better speed than Cressy-class
- Good range for trade protection
- Imposing appearance

## Ships and Fates

1. **HMS Drake** - **SUNK** 1917 (torpedo damage, Ireland)
2. **HMS Good Hope** - **SUNK** 1914 (Battle of Coronel)
3. **HMS Leviathan** - Scrapped 1920
4. **HMS King Alfred** - Scrapped 1920

## Notable Service

**Battle of Coronel (November 1, 1914):**
- HMS Good Hope (flagship) vs German East Asia Squadron
- Admiral Cradock killed when Good Hope exploded
- No survivors from Good Hope
- British defeat, avenged at Falklands

**HMS Drake Loss:**
- Damaged by torpedo from UC-79 (October 2, 1917)
- Attempted to reach port
- Sank off Northern Ireland
- 18 crew lost

**WWI Service:**
- Trade protection
- Northern Patrol
- Atlantic operations
- Training duties

## Legacy

**Design Assessment:**
- Successful armored cruiser design
- Good balance of speed and firepower
- Better than Cressy-class
- Still vulnerable to modern threats

**Combat Record:**
- Two lost in WWI
- Good Hope famous loss at Coronel
- Drake demonstrated submarine threat
- Remaining ships served through war
