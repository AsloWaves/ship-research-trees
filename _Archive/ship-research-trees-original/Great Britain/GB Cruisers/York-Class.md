# York-Class Heavy Cruisers (1930)

## Overview
The York-class were scaled-down County-class heavy cruisers designed for economy while maintaining fighting power. Two ships featured six 8-inch guns and reduced protection, serving as an economical alternative to the expensive County-class. Both were lost in WWII.

## Design

**Economy Heavy Cruiser:**
- Smaller than County-class
- Six 8-inch guns (vs eight)
- Reduced protection
- Cost savings emphasis

**Key Features:**
- Three twin 8-inch turrets
- Good speed (32.5 knots)
- Extended range (10,000 nm)
- Light construction

**Design Philosophy:**
- Affordable heavy cruiser
- Maintain firepower
- Accept reduced protection
- Build more ships for less cost

## Ships

1. **HMS York** - **SUNK** 1941 (explosive boats, Crete)
2. **HMS Exeter** - **SUNK** 1942 (Battle of Java Sea)

## Notable Service

**HMS Exeter at River Plate (December 13, 1939):**
- Engaged Graf Spee with Ajax and Achilles
- Heavily damaged but survived
- Critical role in forcing Graf Spee to Montevideo
- Major British propaganda victory

**HMS Exeter at Java Sea (February 27-28, 1942):**
- Part of ABDA force
- Heavily damaged in battle
- Sunk by Japanese cruisers next day
- 652 crew lost

**HMS York Loss (March 26, 1941):**
- Damaged by Italian explosive boats in Suda Bay, Crete
- Beached to prevent sinking
- Further damaged by air attacks
- Abandoned and destroyed May 1941
