# Early Destroyers (1902-1910)

## Bainbridge-Class Destroyers (DD-1 to DD-13, 1902-1903)

### General Specifications
- **Ships Built**: 13
- **Commissioned**: 1902-1903
- **Decommissioned**: 1919
- **Displacement**: 420 tons (normal), 630 tons (full load)
- **Length**: 250 ft 0 in - 250 ft 6 in (76.0-76.35 m)
- **Beam**: 23 ft 1 in (7.04 m)
- **Draft**: 6 ft 6 in (1.98 m)
- **Crew**: 75 (3 officers, 72 enlisted)

### Armament

**Main Guns:**
- 2× 3-inch/50 caliber guns (76mm) Mark 2
- Type: Low-angle rapid fire
- Mounting: Single pedestal mounts

**Secondary Guns:**
- 5× 6-pounder guns (57mm)
- Mark: Marks 1-13 series (Hotchkiss-derived)
- Type: Rapid fire anti-torpedo boat defense

**Torpedoes:**
- 2× 18-inch (457mm) torpedo tubes
- Configuration: Two trainable single mounts
- Torpedoes Carried: 4 total

### Propulsion

**Engines:**
- Type: 2× Vertical inverted triple-expansion steam engines
- Power: 7,000-8,400 ihp (depending on subclass)
- Shafts: 2
- Design Speed: 29 knots
- Trial Speed: 29.7 knots (USS Stewart)

**Boilers:**
- Number: 4
- Type: Thornycroft, Normand, or Seabury (varied by subclass)
- Pressure: 275 psi (1,900 kPa)
- Fuel: Coal (115-213 tons capacity)

### Fire Control Systems
- No dedicated FCS
- Basic optical telescopic sights
- Manual rangefinding
- Manual gun laying

### Upgrades & Modifications
- **1906**: Some vessels had 3-inch guns replaced with 6-pounders for weight savings
- **WWI (1917-1918)**:
  - 1-2 depth charge racks added for convoy escort
  - Torpedo tube reconfiguration on some ships (twin mounts)

## Smith-Class Destroyers (DD-17 to DD-21, 1909-1910)

### General Specifications
- **Ships Built**: 5
- **Commissioned**: 1909-1910
- **Displacement**: 700 tons (normal), 902 tons (full load)
- **Length**: 293 ft 10 in (89.56 m)
- **Beam**: 26 ft 0 in (7.92 m)
- **Draft**: 8 ft 0 in - 10 ft 11 in (max)
- **Crew**: ~87

### Armament

**Main Guns:**
- Original: 5× 3-inch/50 caliber guns (76mm) Mark 3, 5, 6, or 19
- 1916 Modification: 4× 3-inch/50 caliber guns (one removed)
- Type: Low-angle rapid fire
- Mounting: Single pedestal mounts

**Torpedoes:**
- Original: 3× 18-inch (457mm) torpedo tubes (6 torpedoes)
- 1916 Modification: 2× twin torpedo tube mounts (no reloads)

### Propulsion

**Engines:**
- Type: Parsons direct-drive steam turbines (FIRST US TURBINE DESTROYERS)
- Power: 10,000 shp
- Shafts: 3 (triple-screw)
  - High-pressure turbine: Center shaft
  - Low-pressure turbines: Two outboard shafts
  - Cruising turbines: Fitted on outboard shafts
- Design Speed: 28 knots
- Trial Speed: 30.41 knots (USS Flusser)
- Maximum RPM: 724 rpm

**Boilers:**
- Number: 4
- Type: Mosher coal-fired boilers
- Fuel: Coal (LAST COAL-FIRED US DESTROYERS)
- Fuel Capacity: 304 tons (first ocean-going capability)

### Fire Control Systems
- No dedicated FCS
- Basic optical sights
- Manual rangefinding becoming available ~1905-1910
- Manual gun laying

### Upgrades & Modifications
- **1916**: Major armament modification
  - Single torpedo tubes → 2 twin torpedo tube mounts
  - 5 guns → 4 guns (one removed)
- **WWI (1917-1918)**: 1-2 depth charge racks added

### Historical Significance
- First US turbine-powered destroyers
- Last US coal-fired destroyers
- First true ocean-going destroyers (304-ton coal capacity)
- 50% larger than previous Truxtun-class

# WWI Flush-Deckers (1917-1922)

## Caldwell-Class (DD-69 to DD-74, 1917-1920)

### General Specifications
- **Ships Built**: 6
- **Commissioned**: 1917-1920
- **Displacement**: 1,020 tons (standard), 1,125 tons (normal)
- **Length**: 315 ft 6 in (96.16 m)
- **Beam**: 31 ft 3 in (9.53 m)
- **Speed**: 30-32 knots

### Armament

**Guns:**
- 4× 4-inch/50 caliber guns (102mm)
- 1× 3-inch/23 caliber gun (76mm) AA

**Torpedoes:**
- 12× 21-inch (533mm) Mark 8 torpedoes
- Configuration: 4 triple tube mounts (broadside)

### Propulsion

**DD-69, 70, 71, 74 (Caldwell, Craven, Gwin, Manley):**
- Type: Parsons geared steam turbines
- Power: 20,000 shp
- Shafts: 2
- Speed: 32 knots

**DD-72, 73 (Conner, Stockton):**
- Type: Parsons direct drive turbines
- Power: 18,500 hp
- Shafts: 3
- Speed: 30 knots

**Boilers:**
- DD-69-71, 74: Thornycroft boilers
- DD-72-73: White-Forster boilers

### Fire Control Systems
- Not documented

### Upgrades & Modifications
- **WWII**:
  - Depth charge rack (single, aft)
  - Y-gun projector (forward of aft deckhouse)
- **APD Conversion (USS Manley)**:
  - Redesignated APD-1 (August 1940)
  - Forward stacks and boilers removed
  - Capacity for 200 Marines and four 36-ft Higgins boats
- **Royal Navy Transfer**: 3 ships transferred (Destroyers for Bases Agreement, 1940)

# Interwar Treaty Ships (1934-1943)

## Farragut-Class (DD-348 to DD-355, 1934-1935)

### General Specifications
- **Ships Built**: 8
- **Commissioned**: June 1934 - March 1935
- **Displacement**: 1,500 tons (treaty limit)
- **Length**: 341 ft 3 in (104.01 m)
- **Beam**: 34 ft 3 in (10.44 m)
- **Speed**: 37 knots
- **Range**: 5,980 nm

### Armament

**Guns:**
- 5× 5-inch/38 caliber Mark 12 in Mark 21 single mounts (dual-purpose)
- Forward 2 mounts: Partially enclosed with light armor
- Midships & aft mounts: Open design
- 1943 reduction: 4 guns (midships mount replaced by AA)

**Torpedoes:**
- 8× 21-inch tubes in 2 trainable quad-mounts (centerline)
- Initially Mark 8; upgraded to Mark 15 (1938+)

**AA Upgrades (1943):**
- 4× 40mm Bofors (2×2)
- 5× 20mm Oerlikon

**Depth Charges:**
- 4 K-gun throwers
- 2 stern racks

### Propulsion

**Turbines:**
- 2× Parsons-type reaction turbines (Bethlehem Steel)
- Single-reduction gearing
- Power: 42,800 shp (31,900 kW)

**Boilers:**
- 4× Yarrow boilers
- Pressure/Temp: 400 psi @ 648°F (342°C)
- First US destroyer with superheaters
- Economizers fitted

### Fire Control & Sensors
- Mark 33 dual-purpose director
- SC radar (1943)
- Radio proximity fuses (VT fuses, late 1942)

- **Range**: ~6,500 nm @ 15 knots
- **Crew**: ~273 officers and enlisted

### Armament

**Main Battery:**
- 5× single 5-inch/38 caliber Mark 12 guns
- Mounts: Mark 30 enclosed single mounts (numbered 1-5, bow to stern)
- Mount Weight: 95,700-105,600 lbs (depending on shield type)
- Elevation: -15° to +85°
- Traverse: 328.5°
- Muzzle Velocity: 2,600 fps (initial), 2,500 fps (average)
- Rate of Fire: 15-25 rounds/minute
- Range: 18,200 yards surface, 37,200 ft AA ceiling
- Projectile: 55 lb shell

**Torpedoes:**
- 10× 21-inch torpedo tubes in 2 quintuple mounts (Mark 14/Mark 15 mounts)
- Torpedo: Mark 15 steam turbine torpedo
  - Length: 24 ft, Diameter: 21 in, Weight: 3,850 lbs

**Anti-Aircraft (Varied Throughout War):**
- Initially: Quadruple 1.1-inch/75 cal mount
- Progressively replaced with:
  - 2×4 and 2×2 Bofors 40mm mounts (typical late-war)

  - Range: 11,133 yards, 22,000 ft altitude
  - Projectile: 0.9 kg (1.985 lbs)

  - Rate of Fire: 250-450 rounds/minute (practical 250-320)
  - Effective Range: 1,000 yards vs aircraft

### Propulsion

**Boilers:**
- 4× Babcock & Wilcox "M-type" (divided furnace, air-encased)
- Steam Pressure: 600-615 psi
- Steam Temperature: 850°F

**Turbines:**

- Total Power: 60,000 shp (30,000 shp per turbine)
- Configuration: High-pressure and low-pressure units
- Shafts: 2 propeller shafts

### Fire Control Systems

**Director:**
- Mark 37 Gun Fire Control System
- Computer: Mark 1A Fire Control Computer
- Stabilization: Mark 6 gyroscope (8,500 rpm)

**Fire Control Radar:**
- Mark 12 (rectangular antenna, UHF-Band)
- Height-Finding: Mark 22 "orange peel" (parabolic, 2 ft diameter)
- Combined system designated Mark 12.22
- Earlier ships used Mark 4 radar (replaced ~1944)

**Search Radars:**
- **Surface Search**: SG radar
  - 9-inch PPI display with 15,000 and 75,000 yard range scales
  - 5-inch "A" scan
- **Air/Surface Search**: SC radar
  - Range: 30-75 miles (depending on variant and target)
  - Range accuracy: ±100-200 yards
  - Bearing accuracy: ±3-5°

**Sonar:**
- QCJ sonar (early series)
