**German Optical Excellence** - The Kommandogerät (Command Device) C/38K was the Kriegsmarine's main battery director. Featured 10.5m stereoscopic rangefinder - using binocular depth perception for faster ranging than coincidence types. Superb in daylight, fatally blind at night.

**Key Specs:** 9,500 kg | 10.5m stereoscopic | -10° to +40° | 6°/sec train | 2-axis gyro | 8 crew

**Features:**
- Stereoscopic ranging: Faster than coincidence (3-5 sec vs 8-10)
- Zeiss optics: Exceptional German optical quality
- Heavy armor: 30mm Krupp protection
- Trained operators: Requires exceptional crew skill

**Fatal Flaw:** Poor radar integration. FuMO radars were search-only, never properly integrated with FC. German optical doctrine failed when bad weather/night negated optical advantage. North Cape proved this fatally.

**Combat Record:** Denmark Strait (1941) - ranged Hood at 26km. Channel Dash (1942) - struggled in poor visibility. North Cape (1943) - optical FC useless in Arctic darkness.

**Ships:** Bismarck (3), Tirpitz (3), Scharnhorst (3), Gneisenau (3), Hipper-class (2).
