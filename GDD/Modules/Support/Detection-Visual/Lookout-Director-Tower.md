## Description
Enclosed armored observation and fire control station positioned high on the superstructure. Combines elevated positioning with weather protection and optical equipment. Integrates lookout duties with fire direction, providing both detection and tactical coordination. The pinnacle of visual detection systems.

## Historical Notes
Director towers evolved from simple fire control tops into sophisticated observation posts combining fire direction, visual detection, and tactical coordination. British battleships featured prominent director control towers (DCTs) from the 1920s onward. The King George V class had a distinctive director tower atop the bridge. American battleships used similar fire control stations in their imposing superstructures. These positions provided commanding views but added considerable topweight - a stability concern for designers.
