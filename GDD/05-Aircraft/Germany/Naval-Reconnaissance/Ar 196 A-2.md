## Notes

Standard Kriegsmarine shipboard reconnaissance floatplane throughout WWII. Twin-float monoplane. BMW 132K engine (960hp). Catapult-launched from warships. Replaced He 60 biplane. Could carry 2x 50kg bombs under wings. Excellent sea-handling. Credited with capturing HMS Seal submarine (only U-boat capture of British sub). Production by Arado, SNCASO (France), and Fokker (Netherlands). Served from battleships, cruisers, and coastal bases. Last German floatplane of WWII. Total: 541 built including 15 prototypes, 526 production. Reliable and well-regarded design.
