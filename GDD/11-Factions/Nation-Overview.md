## Vision Statement

Multi-national political warfare system where diplomatic relations dynamically shift based on player actions, creating authentic WWII-era international tensions with reputation consequences that directly impact extraction mission viability and strategic access.

---

## Core Faction Philosophy

### Four Major Powers + Pirates

**Playable Nations**:
1. **United States** - Industrial supremacy and overwhelming production
2. **United Kingdom** - Naval tradition and engineering excellence
3. **Japan** - Innovation and aggressive Pacific tactics
4. **Germany** - Technical superiority and asymmetric warfare
5. **Pirates** - Hostile to all, lawless freedom

**Design Principles**:
- **Distinct Identity**: Each nation has unique strengths, weaknesses, and playstyle
- **Historical Authenticity**: Nations reflect WWII-era capabilities and doctrines
- **Balanced Asymmetry**: Different advantages create rock-paper-scissors dynamics
- **Player Agency**: Actions shape diplomatic relations and reputation
- **Consequence System**: Choices have lasting impact on gameplay experience

**Combined Arms Integration**:
- **Carrier Aviation**: +50% aircraft effectiveness bonus
  - Coordinated strikes with fighters, dive bombers, torpedo bombers
  - Superior carrier operations and flight deck efficiency
- **Overwhelming Firepower**: Multiple target engagement capability
  - Advanced fire control allows engaging 3 targets simultaneously
  - Massed gunfire doctrine (quantity has quality)

  - Can operate far from home ports indefinitely
- **Technological Advantage**: Early access to advanced systems
  - Radar-guided gunfire (+15% accuracy at night)
  - Proximity fused AA ammunition (+40% aircraft damage)

**Strategic Examples**:
- **Central Pacific Campaign**: Coordinate 15+ ship task forces with 300+ aircraft
- **Industrial Warfare**: Economic victory through production superiority
- **Power Projection**: Global operations with self-sufficient logistics

**Fleet Composition Strengths**:
- **Carriers**: Best carrier aviation in game
- **Destroyers**: Reliable, mass-produced, numerous
- **Cruisers**: Strong radar and AA capabilities
- **Battleships**: Massive firepower, excellent fire control

#### Unique Advantages

- **Allied Support**: Bonuses when working with UK forces

#### Strategic Weaknesses
- **Predictable**: Doctrine well-known, tactics anticipated
- **Resource Dependent**: Vulnerable to oil supply disruption
- **Size Disadvantage**: Individual ships less specialized than opponents
- **Spread Thin**: Global commitments reduce local superiority

## Nation Switching & Defection

### Defection System

**When Available**:
- After reaching level 20 (prevents new player confusion)
- Must be in neutral port (cannot defect while at home nation port)
- Cannot defect while in corporation (must leave first)
- One defection allowed per 90 days (prevents abuse)

**Defection Process**:

**Step 1: Declaration Phase (48 hours)**:
- Player declares intention to defect at neutral port
- 48-hour reconsideration window
- Cannot engage in combat during this period
- Can cancel defection without penalty

**Step 2: Financial Penalty**:
- **Base Fee**: 50,000 credits
- **Equipment Confiscation**: Nation-specific items locked
  - Can keep universal items (standard guns, engines)
  - Lose nation-specific technology (radars, special torpedoes)
  - Premium ships may be confiscated (based on value)
- **Total Cost**: Typically 100,000-250,000 credits equivalent

**Step 3: Reputation Impact**:
- **Former Nation**: Reputation drops to -75 (Traitor status)
  - Permanent reputation cap of -50 (can never be truly trusted again)
  - Hostile NPC encounters in former nation waters
  - Port access denied in former nation territories
- **New Nation**: Start at -25 (Suspicious Foreigner)
  - Must prove loyalty through actions
  - Can rebuild to full reputation over time

**Step 4: Integration Requirements**:
- Complete 10 loyalty missions for new nation
- Maintain positive behavior (no friendly fire)
- 30-day probation period before full benefits
- New nation starting ship provided (T1 destroyer)

**Step 5: Strategic Consequences**:
- Permanent restricted access to former nation technology
- Lose faction-specific currency (Resource Points reset to 0)
- Former allies may treat you with suspicion
- Strategic intelligence known to former nation (they know your tactics)

**Example: US ‚Üí Japanese Defection**:
```
Commander Johnson's Defection:

Motivations:
- Prefers Japanese aggressive playstyle
- Wants access to Long Lance torpedoes
- Allied with Japanese player corporation

Financial Costs:
- Base defection fee: 50,000 credits
- Confiscated USS Fletcher: 45,000 credits
- Confiscated US radar: 15,000 credits
- Total Loss: 110,000 credits

Reputation Impact:
- US Navy: +65 ‚Üí -75 (betrayal penalty)
- Japanese Navy: 0 ‚Üí -25 (start as suspicious)
- British Navy: +20 ‚Üí -10 (allied with betrayed nation)
- German Navy: -30 ‚Üí -20 (enemy of former nation, slight improvement)

Integration Process:
- Received IJN Fubuki (T1 starter destroyer)
- Complete 10 Japanese loyalty missions over 14 days
- Reputation climbs to +35 with Japan
- After 90 days: Full integration, access to tech tree

Long-term Consequences:
- Permanently banned from US ports (cannot return home)
- US players may hunt as traitor (bounty system)
- Must rebuild reputation entirely with Japan
- Lost all US-specific research progress
```

**Becoming a Pirate**:
- No fees or confiscation (pirates don't care about paperwork)
- Instant: No loyalty missions required
- Consequences: Hostile with ALL nations permanently
- Cannot defect from pirate faction (one-way choice)
