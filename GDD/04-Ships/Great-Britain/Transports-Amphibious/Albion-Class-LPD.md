**Modern Landing Platform Dock** - Albion-class were Royal Navy's primary amphibious assault ships 2003-2025. Floodable well deck. 405 Royal Marines. Both sold to Brazil 2025.

**Key Specs:** 14,000/21,000 tons | 577ft | 18 kn | 4 LCU + 4 LCVP | 2-4 helicopters | 405 Royal Marines

**Innovation:** Integrated air/sealift capability combining helicopter operations with amphibious assault via floodable well deck. Designed as Fleet Amphibious Flagship with command facilities. Can deploy via 4 LCU Mk 10 landing craft or 2 LCAC hovercraft.

**Ships:**
- **HMS Albion (L14)** - Commissioned June 2003. Maiden East of Suez deployment 2006. Libya operations 2011, Indian Ocean anti-piracy. Fleet Flagship 2015-2023. Decommissioned March 2025.
- **HMS Bulwark (L15)** - Commissioned 28 April 2005. Evacuated 1,300 British citizens from Beirut during 2006 Lebanon crisis (largest British evacuation). Fleet Flagship 2011-2017. Extended readiness 2017-2024. Decommissioned March 2025.

**Service:** Both ships served as Fleet Amphibious Flagship at different periods. Deployed extensively to Mediterranean, Middle East, and Horn of Africa for counter-piracy, counter-terrorism, and expeditionary operations. Notable operations: Libya 2011, Lebanon evacuation 2006, Indian Ocean anti-piracy.

**Controversy:** HMS Bulwark underwent £72.1 million refit 2020-2024 but was decommissioned in November 2024 before returning to service - controversial decision given recent investment.

**Brazil Sale:** Both ships sold to Brazilian Navy 2025. HMS Albion included in letter of intent April 2025. HMS Bulwark officially sold 10 September 2025, becoming NM Oiapoque. Transfer projected by October 2026.
