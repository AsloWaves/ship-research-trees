# Powerful-Class Protected Cruisers (1897)

## Overview
The Powerful-class were the largest protected cruisers ever built, designed to counter foreign armored cruisers. HMS Powerful and HMS Terrible were massive ships with exceptional armament and speed. They served notably during the Boer War and into WWI, demonstrating the limits of protected cruiser design.

## Design

**Largest Protected Cruisers:**
- Designed to counter Russian armored cruisers
- Massive size (14,200 tons)
- Heavy armament
- High speed (22 knots)

**Design Philosophy:**
- Protection through speed and firepower
- Armored deck only
- Ultimately demonstrated need for belt armor
- Too large and expensive

## Ships

1. **HMS Powerful** - Scrapped 1929
2. **HMS Terrible** - Scrapped 1932

## Service

**Boer War (1899-1902):**
- Both ships served off South Africa
- Landed naval brigades with guns
- Provided shore bombardment
- Famous for naval gun crews at Ladysmith

**Pre-WWI:**
- China Station service
- Trade protection
- Diplomatic missions

**WWI:**
- Depot ship roles
- Training duties
- Long service lives (32-35 years)

**Legacy:**
- Demonstrated limits of protected cruiser concept
- Too large for the protection offered
- Influenced shift to armored cruisers
- Last of the giant protected cruisers
