# Iris-Class Protected Cruisers (1878)

## Overview
The Iris-class were the Royal Navy's first protected cruisers, representing a revolutionary design with armored decks instead of side armor. HMS Iris and HMS Mercury were experimental vessels that pioneered the protected cruiser concept, featuring steel hulls and deck armor protecting machinery and magazines.

## Design

**First Protected Cruisers:**
- Revolutionary design concept
- Armored deck instead of side belt
- Steel hull construction
- Protected machinery below waterline

**Innovation:**
- Deck armor over engines and magazines
- Speed and range prioritized over armor
- Pioneered new cruiser design philosophy

## Ships

1. **HMS Iris** - Commissioned 1878, scrapped 1905
2. **HMS Mercury** - Commissioned 1879, scrapped 1904

## Legacy

**Design Impact:**
- Established protected cruiser concept
- Influenced all subsequent protected cruiser designs
- Demonstrated viability of deck armor approach
- Led to 20+ years of protected cruiser construction

**Service:**
- Limited combat service
- Primarily peacetime duties
- Training and experimental roles
