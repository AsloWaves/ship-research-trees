# Arethusa-Class Light Cruisers (1935)

## Overview
The Arethusa-class were small, economical light cruisers designed for trade protection under tight budget constraints. Four ships featured six 6-inch guns and good speed, representing a return to smaller cruiser design. They served extensively through WWII.

## Design

**Small Economy Cruiser:**
- Smallest modern RN cruisers
- Budget constraints driven
- Six 6-inch guns
- Good speed maintained

**Key Features:**
- Compact design
- Twin 6-inch turrets
- Light protection
- Economic construction

**Design Philosophy:**
- Trade protection focus
- Cost savings priority
- Maintain essential capabilities
- Build more ships for less
