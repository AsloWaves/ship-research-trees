# F, G, H & I-Class Destroyers (1934-1937)

## Overview
The F, G, H, and I-classes were the final pre-war flotilla groups built 1934-1937, with 36 ships total (9 per class). They represented the peak development of the standard inter war destroyer before the larger Tribal-class. All served extensively in WWII with heavy losses across all theaters.

## Design Evolution

**F-Class (1934):**
- Enhanced range (6,350 nm)
- Improved habitability
- Better AA armament

**G-Class (1935):**
- Refined F-class
- Superior fire control
- Enhanced seaworthiness

**H-Class (1936):**
- Further AA improvements
- Modernized systems
- Peak flotilla design

**I-Class (1936-1937):**
- Final pre-Tribal standard
- Best range in class
- Ultimate alphabet destroyer

## WWII Service

**Heavy Combat:**
- Narvik battles (H-class particularly)
- Mediterranean extensive service
- North Atlantic convoys
- Arctic operations
- Mediterranean evacuation

**Major Actions:**
- HMS Hardy - 2nd Battle of Narvik, heroic loss
- HMS Glowworm - Rammed Admiral Hipper, VC awarded
- HMS Hunter - Lost 1st Narvik
- HMS Greyhound - Crete evacuation
- Multiple destroyer actions worldwide

## Legacy

**Design Excellence:**
- Peak alphabet-class
- Excellent all-around performance
- Superior range
- Heavy WWII combat

**Historical Significance:**
- Heaviest losses of any RN destroyer group
- Demonstrated courage (Glowworm VC, Hardy at Narvik)
- Backbone of fleet 1939-1942
- Critical early-war period

**Operational Impact:**
- Narvik battles crucial
- Mediterranean workhorses
- Heavy price paid
- Proved design soundness despite losses
