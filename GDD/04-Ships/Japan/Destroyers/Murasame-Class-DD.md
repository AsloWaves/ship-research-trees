**First VLS Destroyers** - Murasame-class are JMSDF's first destroyers with Vertical Launch System. 9 ships commissioned 1996-1999. Compact general-purpose destroyers (6,200 tons). 16 Mk 41 VLS cells (most upgraded to 32). SeaSparrow ESSM, ASROC. One 76mm OTO Melara Super Rapid gun. 8× Harpoon anti-ship missiles. SH-60J/K helicopter. Advanced ASW (OQS-5 sonar, OQR-1 towed array). Crew 165 (automated). Multi-role but ASW-focused. All 9 active as of 2025 (26-29 years service - remarkable longevity). First modern Japanese destroyers to use VLS technology. Mix of US (VLS, radar) and European (OTO gun) systems. Workhorses of JMSDF - proven reliable design serving decades.

**Key Specs:** 4,550/6,200 tons | 495ft | 30 kn | 16-32× VLS | 1× 76mm OTO | OQS-5 sonar | 1× SH-60 | 9 ships active

**Innovation:** Revolutionary for JMSDF (1996-1999). First Japanese destroyers with Vertical Launch System (Mk 41 VLS). VLS advantages: Faster reload than rail launchers, multiple missile types mixed, better magazine protection. Originally 16 cells, most upgraded to 32 cells (improved capacity). SeaSparrow ESSM quad-packed (64-128 missiles possible). ASROC VL (anti-submarine). One 76mm OTO Melara Super Rapid gun (120 rounds/minute, Italian design). 8× Harpoon SSM canisters (later ships Type 90 SSM). Two Phalanx CIWS. OQS-5 hull sonar (advanced for 1990s). OQR-1 TACTASS towed array. SH-60J Seahawk helicopter. COGAG propulsion (4× gas turbines, 60,000 shp, 30 knots). Crew 165 (highly automated - small for 6,200-ton ship). OYQ-9 combat system. Multi-role: ASW primary (excellent sonar, helicopter, ASROC), AAW (ESSM), ASuW (Harpoon). 9 ships built 1996-1999. All active 2025 (26-29 years service, expected through 2030s).

**Design:** Compact destroyer hull (495 ft, 6,200 tons). One 76mm OTO Melara Super Rapid gun forward (fast-firing, multi-role). 16-cell Mk 41 VLS forward of bridge (later upgraded to 32 cells on most ships). SeaSparrow ESSM quad-packed (point defense), ASROC VL (ASW). 8× Harpoon SSM canisters midships (later ships: Type 90 SSM - Japanese indigenous). Two Phalanx Block 1B CIWS. Two triple torpedo tubes (324mm, HOS-303). Helicopter hangar and flight deck aft (SH-60J/K). OPS-24 3D air search radar (rotating). OPS-28C surface search. Type 2-21 fire control radar. OQS-5 hull-mounted sonar (active/passive). OQR-1 TACTASS towed array (passive long-range). COGAG propulsion (4 gas turbines, 2 shafts). Crew 165 (automation reduces manning). Range 4,500 nm. ASW-focused design: Excellent sonar, helicopter with dipping sonar/torpedoes, ASROC, quiet machinery.

**First VLS for JMSDF:**

**Revolutionary Technology (1996):**
- Before Murasame: Missiles launched from rail launchers (Mk 13, etc.)
- **Murasame: Mk 41 VLS** (first time for JMSDF)

**VLS Advantages:**
1. **Faster reload:** Load from below deck (vs topside reload for launchers)
2. **Multiple missile types:** Mix ESSM, ASROC, others in same system
3. **Higher capacity:** More missiles in less space
4. **Better protection:** Missiles stored below deck (armored magazines)
5. **Faster engagement:** Can fire any cell instantly (no launcher rotation)

**Original: 16 Cells**
- Forward of bridge
- Limited capacity
- But proved concept

**Upgraded: 32 Cells**
- Most ships retrofitted
- Doubled capacity
- 32 cells × 4 ESSM = 128 missiles (quad-packed)
- Or: 24× ESSM (96 total) + 8× ASROC = balanced loadout

**Impact:**
- All subsequent JMSDF destroyers use VLS
- Murasame pioneered technology for Japanese Navy

**76mm OTO Melara Gun:**

**European Choice:**
- Italian OTO Melara Super Rapid
- 76mm / 62 caliber
- **120 rounds per minute** (very fast!)
- Fully automatic

**Why Not 5-inch:**
- Aegis destroyers: 5-inch Mk 45 (heavy gun)
- Murasame: 76mm OTO (lighter, faster-firing)
- Trade-off: Less punch per round, but more rounds
- Good for: Small craft, missiles, aircraft, shore bombardment

**Multi-Role:**
- Anti-air: Fast firing against aircraft/missiles
- Anti-surface: Against small craft, patrol boats
- Shore support: High rate of fire
- Ammunition types: HE, SAPOM, guided rounds (DART, Vulcano)
