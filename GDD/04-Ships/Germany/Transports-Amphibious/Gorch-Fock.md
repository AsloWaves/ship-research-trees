**German Sail Training Ship** - Gorch Fock (Type 441) is German Navy's sail training ship. Built 1958 by Blohm & Voss. Named after writer Johann Kinau (died Battle of Jutland 1916).

**Key Specs:** 1,760 tons | 89.32m | Three-masted barque | ~270 crew/cadets | ~17 kn under sail

**Innovation:** Post-war German sail training ship. Three-masted barque (square-rigged on fore and main masts, fore-and-aft rigged on mizzen). Over 14,500 cadets trained since 1958. Named after writer Johann Kinau (pen name "Gorch Fock"), died Battle of Jutland 31 May 1916. Successor to Gorch Fock I (1933, became Soviet Tovarishch 1945).

**Design:** Built Blohm & Voss, Hamburg. Steel hull, full-rigged sailing vessel with auxiliary diesel engine. Traditional sail training emphasizing seamanship, navigation, teamwork. White hull with characteristic tall masts.

**Service:** Deutsche Marine active service 1958-present. Training cruises worldwide. Goodwill ambassador for Germany. Major refit 2015-2021 (prolonged, controversial due to cost overruns). Returned to service 2021. Continues active training mission.
