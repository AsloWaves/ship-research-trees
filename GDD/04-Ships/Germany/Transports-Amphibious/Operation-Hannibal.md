**Largest Maritime Evacuation** - Operation Hannibal (Jan-May 1945) was massive evacuation operation from East Prussia. 494-1,080 vessels evacuated 800,000-900,000 civilians + 350,000 soldiers.

**Key Stats:** 494-1,080 vessels of all types | 800,000-900,000 civilians evacuated | 350,000 soldiers | 158 merchant ships lost

**Innovation:** Largest maritime evacuation in history. German Navy and civilian vessels evacuated over 1 million people from advancing Soviet forces in East Prussia (Pillau, Gotenhafen, Danzig, Königsberg, Libau, Kolberg). Massive humanitarian effort despite ongoing combat operations. Rescued civilians and military personnel from Soviet advance January-May 1945.

**Notable Vessels:** **Wilhelm Gustloff** - sunk by Soviet submarine S-13, 30 Jan 1945, ~9,343 dead (largest maritime disaster in history). **Goya** - sunk 16 Apr 1945, ~7,000 dead. **General von Steuben** - sunk 10 Feb 1945. **Cap Arcona** - sunk by RAF 3 May 1945. Deutschland, Monte Rosa, and numerous other passenger liners, freighters, ferries, naval vessels.

**Service:** Operation conducted January-May 1945 from Courland Pocket and East Prussian ports to western Germany (Kiel, Flensburg, Copenhagen). Despite horrific losses (Wilhelm Gustloff, Goya, General von Steuben), operation rescued over 1 million people—success rate given circumstances remarkable. Kriegsmarine vessels including Tsingtau, various destroyers, torpedo boats, minesweepers, S-boats, plus requisitioned civilian vessels participated. Final convoys departed 8-9 May 1945 as war ended.
