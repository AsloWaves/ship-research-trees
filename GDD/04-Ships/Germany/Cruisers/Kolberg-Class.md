**First L/45 Gun Light Cruisers** - 4 ships built (1910): Kolberg, Mainz, Cöln, Augsburg. First German light cruisers with improved 10.5cm SK L/45 guns (longer barrels, better accuracy). Faster (27.5 knots vs. 24.1), heavier armament (12× guns vs. 10×). 4,362 tons. **Battle of Heligoland Bight** (28 Aug 1914): SMS Mainz sunk by HMS Liverpool (89 killed, 284 survivors), SMS Cöln sunk by British battlecruisers (512 killed, no survivors). SMS Kolberg and Augsburg survived WWI.

**Key Features:** First 10.5cm SK L/45 guns (improved accuracy) | 27.5 knots (fastest to date) | Heligoland Bight: Mainz (89 killed), Cöln (512 killed) | 12× guns

**Ships:** SMS Kolberg (survived WWI, scrapped 1930), SMS Mainz (sunk Heligoland Bight 28 Aug 1914, 89 killed, 284 survivors), SMS Cöln (sunk Heligoland Bight 28 Aug 1914, 512 killed, no survivors), SMS Augsburg (survived WWI, scrapped 1922)

**Service:** WWI High Seas Fleet operations (1914-1918). Battle of Heligoland Bight (28 Aug 1914): First major naval action of WWI. Mainz: Sunk by HMS Liverpool (89 killed, 284 survivors). Cöln: Attempted rescue of Mainz survivors, sunk by British battlecruisers Invincible and New Zealand (512 killed, no survivors). Kolberg and Augsburg: High Seas Fleet service, Battle of Jutland (1916), surrendered 1918, scrapped 1922-1930.
