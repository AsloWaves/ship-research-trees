**Germany's Most Powerful Armored Cruisers** - 2 ships built (1907-1908): Scharnhorst, Gneisenau. Most powerful (8× 21cm guns) and fastest (23.6 knots) German armored cruisers. 11,616 tons, flagship of Admiral Graf von Spee's East Asia Squadron. **Battle of Coronel** (1 Nov 1914): Decisive German victory, sank HMS Good Hope (926 killed including Admiral Cradock) and HMS Monmouth (735 killed). **Battle of Falkland Islands** (8 Dec 1914): Both ships sunk by British battlecruisers Invincible and Inflexible. Scharnhorst: 860 killed (entire crew, no survivors). Gneisenau: 600 killed (187 survivors). Famous for von Spee's tragic final stand.

**Key Features:** Most powerful German armored cruisers (8× 21cm guns) | Coronel victory (1 Nov 1914) | Falklands defeat (8 Dec 1914) | Scharnhorst: 860 killed, no survivors

**Ships:** SMS Scharnhorst (sunk Falklands 8 Dec 1914, 860 killed, no survivors), SMS Gneisenau (sunk Falklands 8 Dec 1914, 600 killed, 187 survivors)

**Service:** East Asia Squadron flagship (1909-1914). Battle of Coronel (1 Nov 1914): Decisive victory over British cruisers Good Hope and Monmouth (1,661 British killed, 0 German casualties). Battle of Falkland Islands (8 Dec 1914): Outgunned by British battlecruisers, Scharnhorst sunk first (860 killed, entire crew), Gneisenau scuttled after heavy damage (600 killed, 187 survivors). Admiral Graf von Spee and both sons killed aboard Scharnhorst. Most famous German armored cruiser action of WWI.
