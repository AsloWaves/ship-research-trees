**Enhanced Light Cruiser Design** - 4 ships built (1907-1908): Königsberg, Nürnberg, Stuttgart, Stettin. Improved Dresden design with steam turbines (24.1 knots). 3,400 tons, 10× 10.5cm guns. **SMS Königsberg**: Famous East Africa raider, sank HMS Pegasus (20 Sep 1914, Zanzibar), trapped in Rufiji Delta (Oct 1914), hunted for 8 months, scuttled after bombardment (11 Jul 1915). **SMS Nürnberg**: East Asia Squadron, sunk at Battle of Falkland Islands (8 Dec 1914, 327 killed, 7 survivors). Stuttgart and Stettin survived WWI.

**Key Features:** Königsberg: East Africa raider, trapped Rufiji Delta | Nürnberg: Sunk at Falklands (327 killed) | Improved Dresden design | Steam turbines

**Ships:** SMS Königsberg (scuttled Rufiji Delta 11 Jul 1915), SMS Nürnberg (sunk Falklands 8 Dec 1914, 327 killed, 7 survivors), SMS Stuttgart (survived WWI, scrapped 1920), SMS Stettin (survived WWI, scrapped 1929)

**Service:** WWI commerce raiding and fleet operations (1914-1918). Königsberg: East Africa raider, sank HMS Pegasus (20 Sep 1914), trapped Rufiji Delta (Oct 1914), bombarded by monitors Severn and Mersey (6 Jul and 11 Jul 1915), scuttled (11 Jul 1915). Nürnberg: East Asia Squadron, Battle of Falklands (8 Dec 1914, sunk by HMS Kent, 327 killed, 7 survivors). Stuttgart and Stettin: High Seas Fleet service, surrendered 1918, scrapped 1920-1929.
