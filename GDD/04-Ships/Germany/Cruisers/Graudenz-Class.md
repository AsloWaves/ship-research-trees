**Heavy 15cm Armed Light Cruisers** - 2 ships built (1914): Graudenz, Regensburg. First purpose-built German light cruisers with 15cm guns (Pillau was ex-Russian). Heavy armament: **12× 15cm guns** (most heavily-armed German light cruisers to date). 4,912 tons, 28.2 knots. Both survived WWI. **SMS Graudenz**: Transferred Italy 1920 (war prize), served as Ancona (1920-1937), scrapped. **SMS Regensburg**: French war prize 1920, served as Strasbourg (1920-1944), scuttled by Germans 1944.

**Key Features:** Heavy 15cm armament (12× guns) | First purpose-built 15cm German cruisers | Both survived WWI | Graudenz: Italian Ancona | Regensburg: French Strasbourg

**Ships:** SMS Graudenz (transferred Italy 1920 as Ancona, scrapped 1937), SMS Regensburg (French war prize 1920 as Strasbourg, scuttled by Germans 1944)

**Service:** WWI High Seas Fleet (1914-1918). Battle of Jutland (31 May 1916): Both ships participated, no major damage. Battle of Dogger Bank (24 Jan 1915): Graudenz participated. Both survived WWI, surrendered 1918. Graudenz: Transferred Italy 1920 (war prize), served as Ancona (1920-1937), scrapped. Regensburg: French war prize 1920, served as Strasbourg (1920-1944), scuttled by Germans in Toulon (1944). Graudenz-class validated heavy 15cm armament for German light cruisers.
