**First German Battlecruiser** - Germany's response to HMS Invincible. Revolutionary balanced design: 250mm armor (+64% thicker than Invincible) with only 0.25 knots slower. First German turbine capital ship. Sank HMS Indefatigable at Jutland, survived 4 heavy hits. Vindicated German armor-first philosophy.

**Key Features:** First German battlecruiser | First German turbines | 250mm belt armor | Balanced design philosophy

**Ships:** SMS Von der Tann (only ship of class)

**Service:** Sank HMS Indefatigable at Jutland (1916). Survived 4 heavy hits, all turrets damaged. Scuttled Scapa Flow (21 June 1919). Raised 1930, scrapped 1931-1934 (last WWI German battlecruiser scrapped).
